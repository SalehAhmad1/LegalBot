The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
the National Memorial Arboretum, Staffordshire, by reason of a dedication service for the 
National Police Memorial. The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of 
the powers conferred by article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (National 
Memorial Arboretum) Regulations 2021 and come into force on 28th July 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0800 hours and 1400 hours on 28th July 2021 no 
aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 2.5 nautical miles whose centre is at 524342N 0014345W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft operating by or on behalf of— 
(a) the National Police Service, or 
(b) the Helicopter Emergency Services.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of the National Memorial 
Arboretum, Staffordshire, during a dedication service for the National Police Memorial due to take 
place there on 28th July 2021. The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport 
agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and 
security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
