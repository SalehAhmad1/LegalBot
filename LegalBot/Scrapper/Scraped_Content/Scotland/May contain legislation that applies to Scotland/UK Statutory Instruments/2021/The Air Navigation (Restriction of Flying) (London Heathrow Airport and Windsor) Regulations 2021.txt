The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
London Heathrow Airport and Windsor, Berkshire, by reason of the intended visit by a Head of 
State. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (London 
Heathrow Airport and Windsor) Regulations 2021 and come into force on 13th June 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations restrict flying in the vicinity of London Heathrow Airport and Windsor, 
Berkshire on the occasion of the intended visit by a Head of State on 13th June 2021. 
As part of the overall security arrangements and as advised by the Metropolitan Police Service, the 
Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that it is in the public interest to 
restrict flying in the areas specified in these Regulations. 
The times mentioned in regulations 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one 
hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to regulation 3(2)(b)(i), the Denham Local Flying Area is the area comprising the 
portion of the aerodrome traffic zone contained within the London Control Zone defined in the 
United Kingdom Aeronautical Information Publication EGLD AD 2.22. 
In relation to regulation 3(2)(b)(ii), the Fairoaks Local Flying Area is the area comprising the 
aerodrome traffic zone defined in the United Kingdom Aeronautical Information Publication 
EGTF AD 2.22. 
In relation to regulation 3(2)(b)(iii), the White Waltham Local Flying Area is the area comprising 
the portion of the aerodrome traffic zone contained within the London Control Zone defined in the 
United Kingdom Aeronautical Information Publication EGLM AD 2.22. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(d), the Metropolitan Police Service may 
be contacted on telephone number 07785370911. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice To Airmen (NOTAM).
