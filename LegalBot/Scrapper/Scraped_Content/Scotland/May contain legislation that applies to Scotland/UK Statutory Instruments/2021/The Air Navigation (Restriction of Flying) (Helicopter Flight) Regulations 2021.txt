The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
vicinity of a route between Greater London and the Solent estuary by reason of an intended 
Helicopter Flight. 
The Secretary of State makes these Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 
of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Helicopter 
Flight) Regulations 2021 and come into force on 22nd May 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace

aircraft is to fly below 4,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by—

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in airspace in the vicinity of Greater London, 
Surrey, West Sussex and Hampshire by reason of an intended Helicopter Flight. Accordingly, the 
Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that it is in the public interest and 
flight safety to restrict flying in the vicinity of the relevant areas to avoid the risk that transient 
aircraft may disrupt the flight.  
The times mentioned in this instrument are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
