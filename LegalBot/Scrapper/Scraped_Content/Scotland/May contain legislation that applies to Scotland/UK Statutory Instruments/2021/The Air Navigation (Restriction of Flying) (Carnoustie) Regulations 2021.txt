The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
Carnoustie, Angus, Scotland by reason of the security operation associated with the intended 
holding of the Women’s Open Golf Championships 2021. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Carnoustie) 
Regulations 2021 and come into force on 15th August 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0400 hours and 2200 hours on each day beginning on 
15th August 2021 and ending on 23rd August 2021 no aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above 
mean sea level within the area bounded successively by straight lines joining— 
(a) 563214N 0023927W, 
(b) 562940N 0024910W, 
(c) 562630N 0024615W, 
(d) 562910N 0023655W, and 
(e) 563214N 0023927W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operating by or on behalf of — 
(i) Police Scotland, 
(ii) the Scottish Air Ambulance Service, or 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations. 
(b) In these Regulations, Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Carnoustie, Angus, Scotland as 
part of the security arrangements for the Women’s Open Golf Championships 2021. Police 
Scotland considers that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the Championships for 
security reasons, and this has been agreed with by the Civil Aviation Authority and Department 
for Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the Police Scotland Aviation Safety 
and Security Unit can be contacted via OSDAviationSafety@scotland.pnn.police.uk or if urgent 
on telephone number 01786 895840. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM). 
The point of contact in the Civil Aviation Authority is Mr S J Maxted, Airspace Regulator, Safety 
and Airspace Regulation Group, on telephone number 03301 383216.
