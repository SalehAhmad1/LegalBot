The Secretary of State has previously decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
vicinity of central Scotland by reason of the 26th Conference of the Parties to the United Nations 
Framework Convention on Climate Change (COP26) that is taking place at the Scottish Event 
Campus (SEC) Glasgow.  
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (COP26) 
(Amendment) Regulations 2021 and they come into force on 11th November 2021. 
Amendment 
2.—(1) The Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (COP26) Regulations 2021(b) are amended 
as follows. 
(2) In regulation 3(1), 3(2), 3(3) and 3(4) for “13th November 2021” substitute “14th November 
2021”.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations amend the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (COP26) Regulations 2021 
(S.I. 2021/1068). They extend the Regulations by one day due to the extension of the 26th 
Conference of the Parties to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change 
(COP26) held at the Scottish Event Campus (SEC) Glasgow.  
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
