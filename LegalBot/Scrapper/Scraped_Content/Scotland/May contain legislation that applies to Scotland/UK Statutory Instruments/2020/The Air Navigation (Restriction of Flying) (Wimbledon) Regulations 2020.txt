The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
Wimbledon, London Borough of Merton, by reason of the gathering of a large number of people 
at the 2020 Wimbledon Lawn Tennis Championships. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Wimbledon) 
Regulations 2020 and come into force on 27th June 2020. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0500 hours and 2200 hours, on each of the seventeen 
days beginning on 27th June 2020 and ending on 13th July 2020, no aircraft is to fly below 1,500 
feet above mean sea level within the area of a circle having a radius of 0.75 nautical miles whose 
centre is 512601N 0001250W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of the National Police Air Service, 
(b) operated by or on behalf of the Helicopter Emergency Medical Services, or 
(c) making an approach to, or departing from London Heathrow Airport whilst under the 
control of the London Terminal Control Centre Swanwick, or the air traffic control unit at 
London Heathrow Airport, or 
(d) flying in accordance with the permission of the Championships Safety Officer.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of The All England Lawn Tennis 
Club, Wimbledon, on the occasion of the 2020 Wimbledon Lawn Tennis Championships which 
are scheduled to take place there from 27th June to 13th July 2020 (inclusive). This event will be 
open to the general public, and large crowds are expected to attend this event each day. The 
Championships Safety Officer has therefore requested that restrictions be imposed on flying in the 
vicinity of the Championships on public safety and security grounds. This has been agreed by the 
Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport. 
In relation to regulation 3, the term ‘aircraft’ includes aircraft as classified by Part 1 of Schedule 4 
to the Air Navigation Order 2016. It also includes, by virtue of article 23 of that Order, any small 
balloon, any kite weighing not more than two kilograms, any small unmanned aircraft and any 
parachute including a parascending parachute. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC), which for the 
purpose of these Regulations is one hour behind British Summer Time. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)d, the Championships Safety Officer 
may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice To Airmen (NOTAM).
