The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary and in the public interest to restrict flying in 
the vicinity of Edinburgh, by reason of the intended holding of events associated with the New 
Year celebrations which will be attended by a large number of persons. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Edinburgh) 
Regulations 2019 and come into force on 30th December 2019. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(2) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (3), and during the periods specified in paragraph (2), no aircraft is 
to fly below 2,200 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius 
of 2 nautical miles whose centre is at 555705N 0031106W. 
(2) The periods referred to in paragraph (1) are— 
(a) between 1500 hours and 2300 hours on 30th December 2019, and 
(b) between 1400 hours on 31st December 2019 and 0300 hours on 1st January 2020. 
(3) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of— 
(i) Police Scotland, 
(ii) the Scottish Air Ambulance Service, or 
(iii) the Maritime and Coastguard Agency,

(b) In these Regulations, Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is the same as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in airspace in the vicinity of Edinburgh for a 
number of events associated with the 2019/2020 New Year celebrations which will be attended by 
a large number of persons. The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree 
that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the events for reasons of public safety and 
security. 
In relation to regulation 3, the term ‘aircraft’ includes aircraft as classified by Part 1 of Schedule 4 
to the Air Navigation Order 2016. It also includes, by virtue of article 23 of that Order, any small 
balloon, any kite weighing not more than two kilograms, any small unmanned aircraft and any 
parachute including a parascending parachute. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC), which for the 
purpose of these Regulations is the same as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3)(c), the Police Scotland Air Accidents 
and Incidents Advisor may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
