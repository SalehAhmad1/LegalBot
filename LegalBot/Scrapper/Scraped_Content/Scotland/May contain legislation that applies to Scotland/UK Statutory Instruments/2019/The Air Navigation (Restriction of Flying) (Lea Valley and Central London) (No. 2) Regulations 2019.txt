The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
vicinity of the Lea Valley, Hertfordshire, and Central London, by reason of the intended visit by a 
Head of State to the North Atlantic Treaty Organization leaders meeting. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Lea Valley and 
Central London) (No. 2) Regulations 2019 and come into force on 2nd December 2019. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulations 3(1), 3(2) and 4(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations restrict flying in a corridor between London Stansted Airport and London 
(regulation 3) and in the vicinity of central London (regulation 4) on the occasion of a visit by a 
Head of State to the North Atlantic Treaty Organization leaders meeting. 
As part of the overall security arrangements for the visit, the Metropolitan Police considers it is in 
the public interest that flying should be restricted in the areas specified in regulations 3(1), 3(2) 
and 4(1) for reasons of public safety and security. This has been agreed with the Civil Aviation 
Authority and the Department for Transport. 
In relation to regulations 3 and 4, the term ‘aircraft’ includes aircraft as classified by Part 1 of 
Schedule 4 to the Air Navigation Order 2016. It also includes, by virtue of article 23 of that Order, 
any small balloon, any kite weighing not more than two kilograms, any small unmanned aircraft 
and any parachute including a parascending parachute. 
The times mentioned in regulations 3(1), 3(2) and 4(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC), 
which for the purpose of these Regulations is the same as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulations 3(3)(c) and 4(2)(c), the Metropolitan Police 
Air Security Coordinator may be contacted at restricted.airspace@met.pnn.police.uk. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
