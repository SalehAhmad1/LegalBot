The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of the AI Safety Summit 
at Bletchley Park. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Bletchley Park) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 20th October 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2)— 
(a) no unmanned aircraft is to fly below 3,000 feet above mean sea level between 2300 hours 
on 20th October 2023 and 2359 hours on 29th October 2023 within the area bounded by a 
circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles centred at 515945N 0004437W, and 
(b) no aircraft is to fly below 3,000 feet above mean sea level between 0001 hours on 30th 
October 2023 and 2359 hours on 3rd November 2023 within the area bounded by a circle 
having a radius of 2 nautical miles centred at 515945N 0004437W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Bletchley Park, 
Buckinghamshire for the AI Safety Summit which is due to take place there. 
In view of the high visibility of this summit and at the request of the Security Services, the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of aircraft should be 
restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Prisons) Regulations 2001 (S.I. 2001/1657, as amended by S.I. 
2007/2377). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST) until 0200 hours on 29th October 2023 and the same time as 
Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) from 0200 hours on 29th October 2023. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b)(iii), the police inspector responsible 
for the security operation may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
