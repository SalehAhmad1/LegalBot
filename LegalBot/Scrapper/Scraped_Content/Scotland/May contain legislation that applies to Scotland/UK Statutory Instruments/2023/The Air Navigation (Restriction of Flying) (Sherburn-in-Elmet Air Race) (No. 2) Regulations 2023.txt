The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of an Aero Club Air Race. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Sherburn-in-
Elmet Air Race) (No. 2) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 19th August 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of the aerodrome at Sherburn-in-
Elmet by reason of the intended holding of an Aero Club Air Race that is due to take place there 
on 19th August 2023 and 20th August 2023. 
Many aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the race and therefore the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 3(2)(a), the Sherburn-in-Elmet Aerodrome 
air ground service can be contacted as notified and the Royal Aero Club Records Racing and Rally 
Association Clerk of the Course may be contacted on telephone number 07961-046582. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM). 
The point of contact in the Civil Aviation Authority is Mr S J Maxted, Airspace Regulator, Safety 
and Airspace Regulation Group, on telephone number 03301 383216.
