The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of a funeral which is expected to take 
place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (St Michael’s on 
Wyre) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 8th March 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1000 hours and 1500 hours on 8th March 2023 no 
unmanned aircraft is to fly below 1,000 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a 
circle having a radius of 1 nautical mile centred at 535146N 0024911W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any unmanned aircraft flying in accordance with a 
permission granted by the Lancashire Constabulary.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of St 
Michael’s on Wyre, Lancashire for a funeral that is scheduled to take place there on 8th March 
2023. In view of the high profile of this funeral and at the request of the Lancashire Constabulary, 
the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned 
aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
time as Greenwich Mean Time. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Lancashire Constabulary may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
