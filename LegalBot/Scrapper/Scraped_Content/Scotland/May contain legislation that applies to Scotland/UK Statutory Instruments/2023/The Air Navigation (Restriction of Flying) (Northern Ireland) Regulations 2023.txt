The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the areas specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there 
of VIP events. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Northern 
Ireland) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 24th May 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace

(a) 2,200 feet above mean sea level between 0700 hours and 1700 hours on the 24th May 
2023 within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles centred at 
543950N 0055402W,

hours on the 25th May 2023 within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 2 
nautical miles centred at 542742N 0060509W,

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Newtownabbey, Hillsborough, Armagh and Enniskillen, Northern Ireland due to VIP events that 
are scheduled to take place there on 24th May 2023 and 25th May 2023. 
In view of the high visibility of these events and at the request of the Police Service of Northern 
Ireland, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of 
unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the events for reasons of public safety and 
security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Police Service of Northern Ireland 
may be contacted on telephone number 02892-600965. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
