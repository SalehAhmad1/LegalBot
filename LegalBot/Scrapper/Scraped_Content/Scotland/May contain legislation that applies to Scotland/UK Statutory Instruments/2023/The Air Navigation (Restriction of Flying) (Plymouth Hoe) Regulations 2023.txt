The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of the 
National Fireworks Championships 2023. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Plymouth Hoe) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 16th August 2023.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Plymouth Hoe, Devon during the intended holding of the National Fireworks Championships 2023 
which are due to take place there between 16th August 2023 and 18th August 2023 (inclusive). 
In view of the high visibility of this event and at the request of Devon and Cornwall Police, the 
Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned 
aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Devon and Cornwall Police drone 
unit may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
