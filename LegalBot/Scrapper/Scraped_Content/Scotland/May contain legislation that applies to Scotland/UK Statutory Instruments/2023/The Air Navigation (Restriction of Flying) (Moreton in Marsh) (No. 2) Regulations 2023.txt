The Secretary of State has decided it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of unmanned aircraft trials. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Moreton in 
Marsh) (No. 2) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 5th February 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(2) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (3), during the periods specified in paragraph (2) no aircraft is to 
fly below 1,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 
1.5 nautical miles centred at 515934N 0014017W. 
(2) The periods referred to in paragraph (1) are between— 
(a) 1100 hours and 1700 hours on 5th February 2024, 
(b) 0830 hours and 1700 hours on each of the three days of 6th February 2024 to 8th 
February 2024, and 
(c) 0830 hours and 1530 hours on 9th February 2024. 
(3) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying in accordance with a permission granted 
by AirCordon Ltd.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Moreton in Marsh during 
unmanned aircraft trials that are scheduled to take place there between 5th February 2024 and 9th 
February 2024 (inclusive). 
Multiple unmanned aircraft will be involved in manoeuvres during the trials and therefore the 
Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in 
the vicinity of the trials for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
time as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3), AirCordon Ltd may be contacted as 
notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
