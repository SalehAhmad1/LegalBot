The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended gathering there of a large number of people 
at the Cheltenham Festival. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Cheltenham) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 14th March 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1015 hours and 2000 hours on each of the four days 
beginning on 14th March 2023 and ending on 17th March 2023 no aircraft is to fly below 3,000 
feet above mean sea level within the area bounded successively by— 
(a) the clockwise arc of a circle having a radius of 3 nautical miles centred on 515533N 
0020316W from 515515N 0020805W to 515331N 0020649W, and 
(b) the anti-clockwise arc of a circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles centred on 515339N 
0021002W from 515331N 0020649W to 515515N 0020805W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Cheltenham, Gloucestershire on 
the occasion of the Cheltenham Festival which is scheduled to take place there from 14th to 17th 
March 2023 (inclusive). In view of the significant number of helicopters expected to operate from 
the temporary heliport at Cheltenham racecourse, as well as the large numbers of people expected 
to attend the event, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that 
flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
