2023 No. 1134 (W. 197) 
ROAD TRAFFIC, WALES 
The A494 Trunk Road (Deeside 
Industrial Park Interchange to the 
Wales/England Border, Flintshire) 
(Temporary Prohibition of Vehicles) 
Order 2023 
Made 
25 October 2023 
      Coming into force                   31 October 2023 
The Welsh Ministers, as traffic authority for the A494 
trunk road, are satisfied that traffic on a specified 
length of the trunk road should be prohibited because 
of works proposed on or near the road. 
The Welsh Ministers, therefore, in exercise of the 
powers conferred upon them by section 14(1) and (4) 
of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(1), make this 
Order. 
Title and commencement 
1. The title of this Order is the A494 Trunk Road 
(Deeside 
Industrial 
Park 
Interchange 
to 
the 
Wales/England 
Border, 
Flintshire) 
(Temporary 
Prohibition of Vehicles) Order 2023 and this Order 
comes into force on 31 October 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In this Order— 
“the trunk road” (“y gefnffordd”) means the A494 
Dolgellau to South of Birkenhead trunk road; 
                                                                               
(1) 
1984 c. 27; section 14 was substituted by the Road Traffic   
(Temporary Restrictions) Act 1991 (c. 26), section 1(1) and 
Schedule 1.  By virtue of S.I. 1999/672, and section 162 of, 
and paragraph 30 of Schedule 11 to, the Government of 
Wales Act 2006 (c. 32), these powers are now exercisable by 
the Welsh Ministers in relation to Wales. 
 
2
“works period” (“cyfnod y gwaith”) means 
intermittent periods commencing at 20:00 on 31 
October 2023 and ending when the temporary 
traffic signs are permanently removed. 
Prohibition 
3. No person may, during the works period, drive 
any vehicle on the length of the A494 main eastbound 
carriageway that extends from the nosing of the 
eastbound exit slip road at Deeside Industrial Park 
Interchange to the Wales/England border.  
Application 
4. The prohibition in article 3 applies only during 
such times and to such extent as indicated by traffic 
signs.  
Duration of this Order 
5. The maximum duration for this Order shall be 6 
months.
