The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying in 
the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of the 2024 
Conservative Party Conference. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Birmingham) 
(No. 2) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 28th September 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0600 hours on 28th September 2024 and 2259 hours 
on 2nd October 2024 no aircraft is to fly below 4,500 feet above mean sea level within the area 
bounded by a circle having a radius of 1 nautical mile whose centre is at 522842N 0015434W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of— 
(i) the National Police Air Service, 
(ii) the Helicopter Emergency Medical Service, 
(b) making an approach to or departure from Birmingham International Airport under the 
control of the air traffic control units at Birmingham International Airport or Prestwick 
Centre, or 
(c) flying in accordance with a permission issued by West Midlands Police.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of the 2024 Conservative Party 
Conference that is due to take place in Birmingham from 28th September to 2nd October 2024, 
inclusive. 
The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be 
restricted in the vicinity of the event location for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(c), West Midlands Police may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
