The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of an unmanned aircraft trial. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Moreton-in-
Marsh) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 8th October 2024.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of aircraft in the vicinity of Moreton-in-Marsh 
during an unmanned aircraft trial that is scheduled to take place there from 8th to 10th October 
2024, inclusive. 
Multiple unmanned aircraft will be involved in manoeuvres during the trial therefore the Civil 
Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the trial for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), AirCordon Ltd may be contacted as 
notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
