The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended gathering there of a large 
number of protected persons. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Burslem) 
Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 12th May 2022.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Burslem, Staffordshire. A large number of protected persons are due to attend an event to be held 
there and Staffordshire Police considers that unmanned aircraft flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. The restrictions have been agreed by 
the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), Staffordshire Police may be contacted 
as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
