The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the airspace 
specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the arrangements associated with the death of Her late 
Majesty Queen Elizabeth II. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Central 
London) Regulations 2022 and they come into force with immediate effect. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraphs (2) and (3), until 0700 hours on 19th September 2022 no aircraft is 
to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded successively by— 
(a) a straight line from 513613N 0001646W to 513611N 0000311W, 
(b) the clockwise arc of a circle having a radius of 17 nautical miles centred on 512812N 
0002713W from 513613N 0000311W to 512013N 0000316W, 
(c) a straight line from 512013N 0000316W to 512015N 0001648W, and 
(d) the clockwise arc of a circle having a radius of 13 nautical miles centred on 512812N 
0000020W from 512015N 0001648W to 513613N 0001646W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations restrict flying in the vicinity of central London and Windsor for a period ending 
with the day of the state funeral of Her late Majesty Queen Elizabeth II. These restrictions form 
part of the overall security arrangements for this event and have been agreed with the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport. These Regulations apply except insofar as 
flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Isle of Dogs) 
Regulations 2004 (S.I. 2004/2091), the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (City of London) 
Regulations 2004 (S.I. 2004/2092), the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Specified Area) 
Regulations 2005 (S.I. 2005/964), and the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Hyde Park) 
Regulations 2017 (S.I. 2017/1300). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST).
