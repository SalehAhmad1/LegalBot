STATUTORY INSTRUMENTS
2022 No. 308
ECCLESIASTICAL LAW, ENGLAND
The Cathedrals Measure 2021 (Saving Provision) Order 2022
Made
14th March 2022
Coming into force in accordance with article 1(2)
The Archbishops of Canterbury and York acting jointly, in exercise of the power conferred by section 53(10) of the Cathedrals Measure 2021(1), make the following Order:
(1)
2021 No. 2.Citation and commencement
1.—(1) This Order may be cited as the Cathedrals Measure 2021 (Saving Provision) Order 2022.
(2) This Order comes into force in relation to each cathedral on the date on which section 17 of the Cathedrals Measure 2021 comes into force in relation to that cathedral.Saving for provision enabling establishment of committees
2.—(1) Section 17 of the Cathedrals Measure 2021 (provision in constitution about committees) does not prevent the inclusion in the constitution of the Chapter of a cathedral of provision enabling the establishment by the Chapter of committees or sub-committees.
(2) For that purpose, section 10(1)(b) of the Cathedrals Measure 1999(1) (which permits the constitution to include provision enabling the establishment of committees) continues in force, despite its repeal by section 51(1) of the Cathedrals Measure 2021; and section 10(1)(b) of the Cathedrals Measure 1999 is to have effect as also enabling the establishment of sub-committees.
(3) A committee or sub-committee established in reliance on paragraph (1) is to be treated for the purposes of the Cathedrals Measure 2021 and the constitution of the cathedral as if it had been established by virtue of section 17(1) of that Measure.

14th March 2022EXPLANATORY NOTE
(This note is not part of the Order)
This Order makes saving provision under section 53(10) of the Cathedrals Measure 2021 in relation to the commencement of section 17 of that Measure. It saves the effect of section 10(1)(b) of the Cathedrals Measure 1999, which permits the constitution of a cathedral to include provision enabling the establishment of committees of the Chapter of a cathedral.
