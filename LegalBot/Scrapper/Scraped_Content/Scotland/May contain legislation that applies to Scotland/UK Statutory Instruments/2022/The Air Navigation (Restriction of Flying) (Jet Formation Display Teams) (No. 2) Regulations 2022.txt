The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
the areas specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of flying displays there by 
a jet formation display team. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Jet Formation 
Display Teams) (No. 2) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 10th August 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), during the period commencing at 0800 hours and ending at 
2000 hours at each of the places specified in column 1 and on the corresponding date or dates 
specified in column 4 of the table in the Schedule no aircraft is to fly— 
(a) in any of the airspace above the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 6 nautical 
miles whose centre is at the point specified in column 2 of the table, and, 
(b) below the corresponding altitude specified in column 3 of the table. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying— 
(a) within controlled airspace or within an aerodrome traffic zone in respect of which there is 
an appropriate air traffic control unit(c) if it flies in accordance with a clearance or 
permission of the unit controlling that airspace or zone, or 
(b) flying in accordance with a permission issued by the relevant flying display director. 
 
 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations. 
(b) Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
(c) “Appropriate air traffic control unit” is defined in Schedule 1 to the Air Navigation Order 2016.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in locations and airspace specified in the Schedule 
during flying displays by jet formation teams such as the Royal Air Force Aerobatic Team, the 
Red Arrows, which are due to take place during the period from 10th August 2022 to 11th 
September 2022 (inclusive). 
In order to protect the display aircraft, other air traffic and spectators, it is considered that flying 
should be restricted in the vicinity of the airspace within which the display teams will perform. 
This has been agreed with by the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the relevant flying display director 
may be contacted on a telephone number which will be notified by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
