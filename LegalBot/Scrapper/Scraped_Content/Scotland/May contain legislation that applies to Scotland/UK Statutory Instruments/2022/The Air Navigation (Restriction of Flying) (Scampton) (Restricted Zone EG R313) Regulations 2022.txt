The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of training flights by the 
Royal Air Force Aerobatic Team, The Red Arrows. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Scampton) 
(Restricted Zone EG R313) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 5th December 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), no aircraft is to fly below 9,500 feet above mean sea level 
within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 5 nautical miles whose centre is at 531828N 
0003303W between the hours of 0830 to 1700 on each of the five days of Monday to Friday every 
week, or as notified by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM)(c) outside these hours. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying under the direction of the Air Traffic 
Control unit at Royal Air Force Waddington. 
Revocation of Regulations 
4. The Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Royal Air Force Scampton) (Restricted Zone EG 
R313) Regulations 2016(d) are revoked.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Scampton, Lincolnshire by 
reason of the intended holding there of training flights by the Royal Air Force Aerobatic Team, 
The Red Arrows (“the team”). In order to protect the team’s aircraft and other air traffic the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should restricted in the 
vicinity of the team’s training area for reasons of public safety and security. These Regulations 
also revoke the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Royal Air Force Scampton) (Restricted 
Zone EG R313) Regulations 2016 (S.I. 2016/14) as these are no longer considered necessary. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated in the United Kingdom Aeronautical 
Information Publication. The restricted zone has been designated as EG R313 for the purpose of 
inclusion in that publication and in related charts and documents. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST) from 1 a.m. on the last Sunday in March and is the same time 
as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) from 2 a.m. on the last Sunday in October.
