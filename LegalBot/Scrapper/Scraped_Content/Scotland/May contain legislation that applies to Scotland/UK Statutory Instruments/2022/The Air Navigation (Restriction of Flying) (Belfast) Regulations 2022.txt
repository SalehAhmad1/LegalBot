The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the visit there by His Majesty King Charles III. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Belfast) 
Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 13th September 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0900 hours and 1600 hours on 13th September 2022 
no aircraft is to fly below— 
(a) 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 1 
nautical mile whose centre is at 543609N 0055544W, and

nautical mile whose centre is at 542740N 0060510W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying above Belfast during the visit by His Majesty 
King Charles III which is scheduled to take place there on 13th September 2022. The Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport have agreed that flying should be restricted 
in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(c), the Police Service Northern Ireland 
may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
