Coming into force with immediate effect 
The Secretary of State has deemed it necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in the Schedule to Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Queen Elizabeth II Bridge, 
West Thurrock) (Emergency) Regulations 2022 by reason of an emergency having arisen there. 
The Secretary of State, in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 of the Air Navigation 
Order 2016(a), makes the following Regulations: 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Queen 
Elizabeth II Bridge, West Thurrock) (Emergency) Regulations 2022 and they come into force with 
immediate effect. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3. No aircraft is to fly below 1,500 feet above mean sea level over the area specified in Column 
1 of the Schedule, being an area bounded by a circle of the radius specified in Column 2 and 
centred on the position specified in Column 3.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying of aircraft in the vicinity of Queen Elizabeth II 
Bridge, West Thurrock. They are intended to be of temporary duration and will be revoked as 
soon as the emergency in question has passed.
