The Secretary of State for a reason affecting the public interest has decided that it is necessary to 
amend the restriction of flying in the vicinity of Auchenbreck, Scotland. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Auchenbreck) 
(No. 6) (Emergency) (Amendment) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 19th July 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3 the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Amendment of the Regulations 
3. In regulation 3(1) of the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Auchenbreck) (No. 6) 
Regulations 2022(c) for “between 0800 hours on 13th July 2022 and 1700 hours on 20th July 
2022” substitute “between 0800 hours on 13th July 2022 and 1700 hours on 28th July 2022”.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations amend the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Auchenbreck) (No. 6) 
(Emergency) Regulations 2022 (S.I. 2022/785) by extending the time period of the restrictions 
from 1700 hours on 20th July 2022 to 1700 hours on 28 July 2022. This has been requested by 
Police Scotland and has been agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for 
Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3 are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
