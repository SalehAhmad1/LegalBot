The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of 
Christmas Market events. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Birmingham) 
(No. 4) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 11th November 2022. 
Interpretation

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Birmingham City centre during the holding of the Christmas Market events that are scheduled to 
take place there beginning on 11th November 2022 and finishing on 23rd December 2022. In view 
of the high visibility of this event and at the request of the West Midland Police Service, the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft 
should be restricted in the vicinity of the events for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3), the West Midlands Police Drone Unit 
may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
