The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended holding of the 1 Big Summer 
Event which is scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Plymouth Hoe) 
Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 2nd June 2022.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Plymouth Hoe, Devon during the holding of the 1 Big Summer Event which is scheduled to take 
place there between 2nd June 2022 and 4th June 2022 (inclusive). In view of the high visibility of 
this event and at the request of Devon and Cornwall Police, the Civil Aviation Authority and 
Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted for 
reasons of safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Devon and Cornwall Police drone 
unit may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
