The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
the places specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended holding of flying displays there by a 
jet formation display team. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Jet Formation 
Display Teams) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 2nd June 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), during the period commencing at 0800 hours and ending at 
2000 hours at each of the places specified in column 1 in the table and on the corresponding date 
or dates specified in column 4 of the table no aircraft is to fly— 
(a) in any of the airspace above the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 6 nautical 
miles whose centre is at the point specified in column 2 of the table, and 
(b) below the corresponding altitude specified in column 3 of the table. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying— 
(a) within controlled airspace or within an aerodrome traffic zone in respect of which there is 
an appropriate air traffic control unit(c) if it flies in accordance with a clearance or 
permission of the unit controlling that airspace or zone, or 
(b) in accordance with a permission issued by the relevant flying display director.

(b) Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in locations and airspace specified in the Schedule 
during flying displays by jet formation teams such as the Royal Air Force Aerobatic Team, the 
Red Arrows, which are due to take place during the period from 2nd June 2022 to 28th July 2022 
(inclusive). 
In order to protect the display aircraft, other air traffic and spectators, it is considered that flying 
should be restricted in the vicinity of the airspace within which the display teams will perform. 
This has been agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST).
