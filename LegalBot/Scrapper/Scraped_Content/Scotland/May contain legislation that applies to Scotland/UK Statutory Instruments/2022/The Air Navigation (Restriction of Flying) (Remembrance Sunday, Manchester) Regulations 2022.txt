The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the holding there of a Remembrance 
Sunday ceremony. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Remembrance 
Sunday, Manchester) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 13th November 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft above Manchester City 
Centre during the Remembrance Sunday ceremony at St Peters Square which is scheduled to take 
place there on 13th November 2022. The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for 
Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the 
event for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Greater Manchester Police Security 
Co-ordinator may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
