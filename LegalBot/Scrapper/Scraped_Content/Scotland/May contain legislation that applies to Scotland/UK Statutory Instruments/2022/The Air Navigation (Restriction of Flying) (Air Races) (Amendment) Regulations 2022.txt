The Secretary of State has previously decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict 
flying in the vicinity of the aerodromes at Old Warden and Eshott by reason of the intended 
holding of the Aero Club Air Races and now considers it necessary in the public interest to amend 
the restriction of flying. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Air Races) 
(Amendment) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 24th September 2022. 
Amendment of the Regulations 
2.—(1) The Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Air Races) Regulations 2022(b) are 
amended as follows. 
(2) In regulation 3(3)— 
(a) omit “Subject to paragraphs (7) and (9), between 1145 hours and 1315 hours and between 
1515 hours and 1645 hours on 23rd July 2022 and 1st October 2022 and between 1015 
hours and 1145 hours and between 1345 hours and 1515 hours on 24th July 2022 no 
aircraft is to fly below 1,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by 
straight lines joining successively the following points— (a) 520904N 0001627W, (b) 
520550N 0002214W, (c) 515944N 0001840W, (d) 520454N 0000549W, and (e)

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations amend the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Air Races) Regulations 2022 
(S.I. 2022/212) by amending the timings and coordinates for the aero race on 1st October 2022 
which is intended to be held in the vicinity of Old Warden and by amending the timings for the 
aero races scheduled to be held at Eshott on 24th and 25th September 2022. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
