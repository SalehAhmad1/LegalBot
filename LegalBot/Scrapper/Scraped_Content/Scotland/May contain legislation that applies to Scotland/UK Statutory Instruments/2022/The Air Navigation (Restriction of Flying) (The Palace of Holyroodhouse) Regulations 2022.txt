The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of a Royal 
Garden party that is scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (The Palace of 
Holyroodhouse) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 29th June 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft above Edinburgh during 
the holding of a Royal Garden party that is scheduled to take place on 29th June 2022 at the Palace 
of Holyroodhouse. The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the 
flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of security 
and public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Police Scotland Air Accident and 
Incident Advisor may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
