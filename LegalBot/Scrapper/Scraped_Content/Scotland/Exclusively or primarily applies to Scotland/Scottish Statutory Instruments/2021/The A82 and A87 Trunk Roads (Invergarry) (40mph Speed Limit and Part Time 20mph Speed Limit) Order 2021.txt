The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
84(1)(a) and (c), and 124(1)(d) of, and paragraph 27 of, schedule 9 of the Road Traffic Regulation 
Act 1984(a) and all other powers enabling them to do so. 
In accordance with section 84(2)(b) of that Act, they have given public notice of their intention to 
make this Order, and have complied with Parts II and III of the Secretary of State’s Traffic Orders 
(Procedure) (Scotland) Regulations 1987(c). 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as the A82 and A87 Trunk Roads (Invergarry) (40mph Speed Limit 
and Part Time 20mph Speed Limit) Order 2021 and comes into force on 16 March 2021. 
Speed restrictions 
2. No person may drive any motor vehicle at a speed exceeding— 
(a) 40 miles per hour on the lengths of road specified in Part 1 of the schedule of this Order, 
or 
(b) 20 miles per hour on the length of road specified in Part 2 of the schedule of this Order 
when that particular speed limit is identified as applying to that length of road by means 
of an appropriate traffic sign of the type described in item 1 (diagram 670 - maximum 
speed limit in mph) of the sign table in Part 2 of schedule 10 of the Traffic Signs 
Regulations and General Directions 2016(d).

Revocation 
3. The Trunk Roads (Route A87) (Invergarry) (40mph Speed Limit) Order 1982(a) and the A87 
Trunk Road (Invergarry) (40mph Speed Limit) Variation and Invergarry Primary School (Part-
time 20mph Speed Limit) Order 2006(b) are revoked. 
Exemption 
4. No speed restriction imposed by this Order applies to— 
(a) vehicles falling within regulation 3(4) of the Road Traffic Exemptions (Special Forces) 
(Variation and Amendment) Regulations 2011(c) when used in accordance with 
regulation 3(5) of those Regulations, or 
(b) vehicles being used in an emergency for coastguard purposes.

Article 2 
SPECIFIED LENGTHS OF ROAD

kerbline of the unclassified U1187 Garry Crescent, a distance of 1.23 kilometres or thereby.
