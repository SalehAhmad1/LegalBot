The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
1(1)(a) and 2(1), (2) and (3) of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(a), and all other powers 
enabling them to do so. 
In accordance with section 1(1)(a) of that Act, they consider it expedient to make this Order for 
avoiding danger to persons or other traffic using the road or any other road or for preventing the 
likelihood of any such danger arising. 
In accordance with section 1(1)(c) of that Act they consider it expedient to make this Order for 
facilitating the passage on the road of any class of traffic (including pedestrians). 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as the A90 Trunk Road (Forth Road Bridge and Queensferry 
Crossing Approach Roads) (Prohibitions on Use and Stopping and Bus Lane) Order 2018 and 
comes into force on 24th April 2018. 
Interpretation 
2. In this Order— 
“A90” means that part of the M90/A90 Edinburgh – Fraserburgh Trunk Road at Ferrytoll 
Junction and from South Queensferry to Dalmeny; 
“M90” means that part of the M90/A90 Edinburgh – Fraserburgh Trunk Road from Ferrytoll 
Junction to Queensferry Junction.

Prohibition on use of road 
3.—(1) No person shall use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on the lengths of road specified in 
Part 1 of schedule 1 of this Order. 
(2) No prohibition imposed by paragraph (1) applies to— 
(a) a bus within the meaning given by section 1 of the Public Passenger Vehicles Act 
1981(a); 
(b) a pedal cycle; 
(c) a vehicle being used in an emergency or responding to an emergency for fire brigade, 
coastguard, ambulance or police purposes; 
(d) a vehicle being used for carrying out any maintenance, inspection, clearance, cleaning, 
repair, alteration or improvement of any part of that road; or 
(e) a vehicle being used for the erection, laying, placing, maintenance, testing, alteration, 
repair or removal of any structure, works, apparatus in, on, under or over any part of that 
road. 
(3) No person shall use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on the length of road specified in Part 
2 of schedule 1. 
(4) No prohibition imposed by paragraph (3) applies to— 
(a) a bus within the meaning given by section 1 of the Public Passenger Vehicles Act 1981; 
(b) a taxi or private hire car within the meaning given by section 23 of the Civic Government 
(Scotland) Act 1982(b); 
(c) a pedal cycle; 
(d) a vehicle being used in an emergency or responding to an emergency for fire brigade, 
coastguard, ambulance or police purposes; 
(e) a vehicle being used for carrying out any maintenance, inspection, clearance, cleaning, 
repair, alteration or improvement of any part of that road; or 
(f) a vehicle being used for the erection, laying, placing, maintenance, testing, alteration, 
repair or removal of any structure, works, apparatus in, on, under or over any part of that 
road. 
Prohibition on stopping 
4.—(1) No vehicle may stop or remain at rest on any part of the lengths of road specified in 
schedule 2. 
(2) Paragraph 1 does not apply where it is necessary for a vehicle which is being driven on the 
carriageway of the road to be stopped— 
(a) because of a breakdown or mechanical defect or lack of fuel, oil or water, required for the 
vehicle; or 
(b) because of any accident, illness or other emergency; or 
(c) to permit any person carried in or on the vehicle to recover or move any object which has 
fallen on the road; or 
(d) to permit any person carried in or on the vehicle to give help which is required by any 
other person in any of the circumstances specified in the provisions of this paragraph. 
(3) Nothing in this article precludes a vehicle from stopping or remaining at rest on a 
carriageway while it is prevented from proceeding along the carriageway by the presence of any 
other vehicle or any person or object.

3
Bus lane 
5.—(1) No person shall use, or permit the use of, any vehicle other than a bus to enter, proceed, 
or wait within the length of road specified in schedule 3. 
(2) For the purposes of paragraph (1) “bus” means a motor vehicle constructed or adapted to 
carry more than 23 seated passengers (exclusive of the driver). 
(3) No prohibition imposed by paragraph (1) applies to— 
(a) any vehicle being used in an emergency for fire brigade, coastguard, ambulance or police 
purposes; 
(b) any vehicle being used in connection with the removal of any obstruction, or in 
connection with authorised works to or in that length of road; or 
(c) any vehicle being used in connection with the maintenance, inspection, repair, cleaning, 
clearance, alteration or improvement of that length of road.

SCHEDULE 1 
Article 3 
LENGTH OF ROAD WITH PROHIBITION ON USE 
PART 1 
1. That length of the A90 being the left hand lane of the circulatory carriageway of the 
roundabout at Ferrytoll Junction from the northbound on-slip road to the M90 to the southbound 
off-slip road from the M90, a distance of 100 metres or thereby. 
2. That length of the A90 being the left hand lane of the circulatory carriageway of the 
roundabout at Ferrytoll Junction from the junction with the B981 Hope Street to the southbound 
on-slip road to the M90, a distance of 40 metres or thereby. 
PART 2 
3. That length of the A90 being the left hand lane of the southbound on-slip road at Ferrytoll 
Junction from its commencement at the roundabout at Ferrytoll Junction southwards for a distance 
of 145 metres or thereby.
