The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
2(1) and (2), and 14(1)(b) and (4) of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(a) and all other powers 
enabling them to do so. 
Citation, commencement and cessation 
1. This Order may be cited as the A956 Trunk Road (Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route) 
(Cleanhill to Charleston) (Temporary Prohibitions and Restrictions) Amendment (No. 2) Order 
2019, comes into force on 30 January 2019 and ceases to have effect on 30 June 2019. 
Amendment of the A956 Trunk Road (Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route) (Cleanhill to 
Charleston) (Temporary Prohibitions and Restrictions) Order 2018 
2.—(1) The A956 Trunk Road (Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route) (Cleanhill to Charleston) 
(Temporary Prohibitions and Restrictions) Order 2018(b) is amended in accordance with 
paragraph (2). 
(2) In article 11— 
(a) in sub-paragraph (a) of paragraph (1), omit the words “as a constable or”, and 
(b) after that paragraph, insert— 
“(1A) Articles 4 to 8 and 10 do not apply to a vehicle being used by any person in the 
exercise of their duty as a constable. 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) 1984 c.27; section 2(1) and (2) were amended by the New Roads and Street Works Act 1991 (c.22) (“the 1991 Act”) 
schedule 8, paragraph 18 and section 14 was substituted by the Road Traffic (Temporary Restrictions) Act 1991 (c.26), 
schedule 1.  The Scottish Ministers are the traffic authority by virtue of section 121A of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 
1984 (inserted by the 1991 Act, schedule 8, paragraph 70 and amended by S.I. 2001/1400) as read with section 151(1) of 
the Roads (Scotland) Act 1984 (c.54) (relevantly amended by S.I. 2001/1400).  The functions of the Secretary of State were 
transferred to the Scottish Ministers by section 53 of the Scotland Act 1998 (c.46). 
(b) S.S.I. 2018/365, as amended by S.S.I. 2019/4  
 
2
(1B) Article 9 does not apply to a vehicle being used by any person for police purposes.”.
