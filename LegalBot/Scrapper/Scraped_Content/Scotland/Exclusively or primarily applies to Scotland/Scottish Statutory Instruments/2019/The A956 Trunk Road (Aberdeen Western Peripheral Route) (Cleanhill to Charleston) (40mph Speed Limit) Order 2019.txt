The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
2(1) and (2), and 84(1)(a) of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(a) and all other powers 
enabling them to do so. 
In accordance with section 84(2)(b) of that Act, they have given public notice of their intention to 
make this Order, and have complied with Parts II and III of the Secretary of State’s Traffic Orders 
(Procedure) (Scotland) Regulations 1987(c). 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as the A956 Trunk Road (Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route) 
(Cleanhill to Charleston) (40mph Speed Limit) Order 2019 and comes into force on 30 June 2019. 
Speed limit 
2.—(1) A person must not drive any motor vehicle at a speed exceeding 40 miles per hour on 
the lengths of road specified in the schedule. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any person using any— 
(a) vehicle falling within regulation 3(4) of the Road Traffic Exemptions (Special Forces)

SCHEDULE 
Article 2 
SPECIFIED LENGTH OF ROAD 
1. That length of the A956 – Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route Trunk Road being the 
eastbound carriageway at Charleston Junction from a point 212 metres or thereby east of the 
centreline of the C5K Schoolhill to Lochton to Nigg Road Overbridge, in a generally easterly 
direction for a distance of 210 metres or thereby. 
2. That length of the A956 – Aberdeen Western Peripheral Route Trunk Road being the 
westbound carriageway at Charleston Junction from a point 422 metres or thereby east of the 
centreline of the C5K Schoolhill to Lochton to Nigg Road Overbridge, in a generally westerly 
direction for a distance of 210 metres or thereby.
