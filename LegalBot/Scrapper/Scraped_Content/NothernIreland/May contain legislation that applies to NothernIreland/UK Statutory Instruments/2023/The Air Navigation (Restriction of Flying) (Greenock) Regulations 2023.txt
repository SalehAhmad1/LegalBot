The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of a maritime recovery operation which is 
scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Greenock) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 10th March 2023.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Greenock, Scotland during the recovery of a vessel that is scheduled to take place there between 
10th March 2023 and 12th March 2023 (inclusive). In view of the high visibility of this recovery 
operation, and at the request of Police Scotland, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department 
for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the 
maritime recovery operation for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
time as Greenwich Mean Time. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), Police Scotland may be contacted as 
notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
