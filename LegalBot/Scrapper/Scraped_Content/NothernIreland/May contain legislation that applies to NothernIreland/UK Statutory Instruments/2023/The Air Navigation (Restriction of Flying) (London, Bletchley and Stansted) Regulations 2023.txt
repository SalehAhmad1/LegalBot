The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
areas specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended transit there by a guest of the United 
Kingdom Government. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (London, 
Bletchley and Stansted) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 2nd November 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3— 
(a) “controlled airspace” has the same meaning as defined in Schedule 1 to the Air 
Navigation Order 2016(b), and 
(b) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(c).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations restrict flying within a corridor between London and Bletchley Park, over 
Bletchley Park, within a corridor between Bletchley Park and London Stansted Airport, and in the 
vicinity of London Stansted Airport on the occasion of a visit by a guest of the United Kingdom 
Government which is due to take place on 2nd November 2023. 
As part of the overall security arrangements for the visit, the Civil Aviation Authority and the 
Department for Transport agree that it is in the public interest that flying should be restricted in the 
relevant areas for reasons of public safety and security. 
 
4
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Prisons) Regulations 2001 (S.I. 2001/1657, amended by S.I. 2007/2377). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3 are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
time as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(4)(d)(ii), 3(6)(b) and 3(8)(c), the 
Metropolitan Police Service may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to
