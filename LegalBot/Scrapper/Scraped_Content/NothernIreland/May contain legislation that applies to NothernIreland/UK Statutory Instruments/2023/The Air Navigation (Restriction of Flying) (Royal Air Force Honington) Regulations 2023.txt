The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of an air show. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Royal Air 
Force Honington) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 27th July 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1200 hours and 1600 hours on 27th July 2023 no 
aircraft is to fly below 5,000 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 4 nautical miles centred at 522036N 0004629E. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) flying in accordance with a permission issued by the Royal Air Force Honington Air 
Show flying display director, or 
(b) making an approach to or departure from Royal Air Force Lakenheath or Royal Air Force 
Mildenhall whilst under the direction of the air traffic control unit at Royal Air Force 
Lakenheath in liaison with the Royal Air Force Honington Air Show flying display 
director.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Royal Air Force Honington, 
Suffolk during air displays that are scheduled to take place there on 27th July 2023. 
Many aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the displays and therefore the 
Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Royal Air Force Honington Air 
Show flying display director may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
