The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
aircraft in the areas specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended visit there by a Head of 
State. 
Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 
239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Stansted and 
Central London) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 9th July 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3 the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations restrict flying in the vicinity of London Stansted Airport, within a corridor 
between London Stansted Airport and London, and in Central London on the occasion of a visit by 
a Head of State which is due to take place on 9th July 2023 and 10th July 2023.

As part of the overall security arrangements for the visit, the Civil Aviation Authority and the 
Department for Transport agree that it is in the public interest that flying should be restricted in the 
relevant areas for reasons of public safety and security. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Isle of Dogs) Regulations 2004 (S.I. 2004/2091), the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (City of London) Regulations 2004 (S.I. 2004/2092), the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Specified Area) Regulations 2005 (S.I. 2005/964), the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Hyde Park) Regulations 2017 (S.I. 2017/1300), and the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Windsor Castle) Regulations 2021 (S.I. 2021/1137). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3 are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 3(3) and 3(6), the Metropolitan Police 
Service may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
