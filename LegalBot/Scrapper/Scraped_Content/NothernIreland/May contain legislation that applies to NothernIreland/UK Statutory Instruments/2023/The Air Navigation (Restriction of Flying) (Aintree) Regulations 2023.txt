The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended gathering there of a large 
number of persons at the Grand National 2023 event. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Aintree) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 13th April 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In Regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of Aintree 
Racecourse, Merseyside on the occasion of the Grand National 2023 event which is scheduled to 
take place there on the three days between 13th and 15th April 2023 (inclusive). In view of the 
large number of persons expected to attend the event, the Civil Aviation Authority and the 
Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of the event 
should be restricted for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Operations Manager at Aintree 
Racecourse may be contacted on telephone number 07931 514339. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
