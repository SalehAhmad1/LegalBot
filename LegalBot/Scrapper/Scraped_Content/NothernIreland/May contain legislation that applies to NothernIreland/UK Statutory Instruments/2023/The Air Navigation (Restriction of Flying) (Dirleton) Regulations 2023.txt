The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of the 2023 Scottish Open 
golf tournament. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Dirleton) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 10th July 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0400 hours and 2100 hours on each of the seven days 
beginning on 10th July 2023 and ending on 16th July 2023 no aircraft is to fly below 2,000 feet 
above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 1 nautical mile 
centred at 560304N 0024842W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of— 
(i) Police Scotland, 
(ii) the Helicopter Emergency Medical Services, or 
(iii) the Maritime and Coastguard Agency for the purposes of search and rescue only, or 
(b) operating with the permission of the Scottish Open event safety officer.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Dirleton, North Berwick by 
reason of the intended gathering there of a large number of persons due to attend the 2023 Scottish 
Open golf tournament and associated events that are due to take place there between the 10th July 
2023 and 16th July 2023 (inclusive). 
At the request of Police Scotland, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport 
agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the tournament for reasons of public safety 
and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the Scottish Open event safety 
officer may be contacted on telephone number 07801 271968. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM). 
The point of contact in the Civil Aviation Authority is Mr David Ridley, Airspace Regulator, 
Safety and Airspace Regulation Group, on telephone number 03301 382471.
