The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended gathering of a large number of persons 
for the Summer and Winter Solstice periods. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Stonehenge) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 20th June 2023.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Stonehenge, Wiltshire during the 
summer and winter solstice periods when it is anticipated there will be a large gathering of persons 
there on 20th June 2023 and 21st June 2023 and 21st December 2023 and 22nd December 2023. 
At the request of Wiltshire Police, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport 
agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of Stonehenge during the summer and winter 
solstice periods for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST) in regulation 3(1)(a) and is the same time as Greenwich Mean 
Time in regulation 3(1)(b). 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 3(2), Wiltshire Police may be contacted as 
notified and the Senior Air Operations Officer at Salisbury Plain Air Operations may be contacted 
on 01980 674729. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
