The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the expected gathering there of a large 
number of persons. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Cannock) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 11th March 2023.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Cannock, Staffordshire during an event involving a large gathering of persons that is scheduled to 
take place there on 11th March 2023. In view of the high visibility of this event and at the request 
of the Staffordshire Police Service, the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport 
agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for 
reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
time as Greenwich Mean Time. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Staffordshire Police Service may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
