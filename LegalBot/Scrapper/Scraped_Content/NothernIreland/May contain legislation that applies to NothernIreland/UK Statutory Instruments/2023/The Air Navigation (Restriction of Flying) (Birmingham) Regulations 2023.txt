The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there 
of Birmingham Pride. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Birmingham) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 27th May 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Birmingham City Centre during Birmingham Pride that is scheduled to take place there on 27th 
May 2023 and 28th May 2023. 
In view of the high visibility of this event and at the request of the West Midland Police Service, 
the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned 
aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the West Midlands Police Service may 
be contacted on telephone number 07387 097782 (West Midlands Police Inspector) or on 
telephone number 07778 470310 (West Midlands Police Sergeant). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
