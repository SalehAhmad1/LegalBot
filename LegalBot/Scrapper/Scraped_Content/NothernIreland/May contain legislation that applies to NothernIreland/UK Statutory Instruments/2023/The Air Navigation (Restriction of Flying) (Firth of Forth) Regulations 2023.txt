The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest and national defence to 
restrict the flying of unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the 
operations of HMS Prince of Wales which are due to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Firth of Forth) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 19th July 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), no unmanned aircraft is to fly below 1,000 feet above mean sea 
level between— 
(a) 0900 hours and 1700 hours on the 19th July 2023 within the area bounded by a circle 
having a radius of 1 nautical mile centred at 560031N 0032616W, and 
(b) 0900 hours and 1700 hours on the 24th July 2023 within the area bounded by straight

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of the 
Firth of Forth because of the operations of HMS Prince of Wales that are due to take place there 
on 19th July 2023 and 24th July 2023. 
In view of the high visibility of these operations and at the request of Police Scotland, the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft 
should be restricted in the vicinity of HMS Price of Wales for reasons of national defence and 
security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Police Scotland Aviation Safety and 
Security Unit may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
