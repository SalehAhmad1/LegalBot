The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
the area specified in regulation 3 by reason of the holding of events to commemorate the Platinum 
Jubilee of Her Majesty the Queen. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Platinum 
Jubilee Events, London) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 2nd June 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0500 hours on 2nd June 2022 and 0059 hours on 6th 
June 2022 no aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a 
circle having a radius of 2.15 nautical miles whose centre is at 513029N 0000741W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the airspace above London during the Platinum 
Jubilee events scheduled to take place between 2nd June and 6th June (inclusive). The Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(c), the Metropolitan Police may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
