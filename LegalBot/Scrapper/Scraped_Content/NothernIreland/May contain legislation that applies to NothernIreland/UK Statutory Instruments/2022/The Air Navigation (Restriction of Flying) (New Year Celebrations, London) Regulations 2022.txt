The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of events associated with New 
Year celebrations which will be attended by a large number of persons. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (New Year 
Celebrations, London) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 31st December 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraphs (2) and (3), between 2000 hours on 31st December 2022 and 0200 
hours on 1st January 2023 no aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the 
area bounded by a circle having a radius of 2.5 nautical miles whose centre is at 513012N 
0000713W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying above London during the New Year Celebrations 
which are scheduled to take place there on 31st December 2022 and 1st January 2023. The Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (City of London) Regulations 2004 (S.I. 2004/2092), the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Specified Area) Regulations 2005 (S.I. 2005/964), and the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Hyde Park) Regulations 2017 (S.I. 2017/1300). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3)(c), the Metropolitan Police may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by an Aeronautical Information 
Publication (AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
