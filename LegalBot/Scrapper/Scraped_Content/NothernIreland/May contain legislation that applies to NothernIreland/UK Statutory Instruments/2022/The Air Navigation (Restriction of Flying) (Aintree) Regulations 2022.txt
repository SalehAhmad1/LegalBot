The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the vicinity of Aintree Racecourse, Merseyside by reason of the intended gathering 
there of large numbers of persons associated with the Grand National Festival 2022. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Aintree) 
Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 7th April 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of Aintree 
Racecourse, Merseyside by reason of the intended gathering there of large numbers of persons at 
the Grand National Festival 2022 event on the three days between 7th and 9th April 2022 
(inclusive). The Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that unmanned 
flying should be restricted for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Operations Manager at Aintree 
Racecourse may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
