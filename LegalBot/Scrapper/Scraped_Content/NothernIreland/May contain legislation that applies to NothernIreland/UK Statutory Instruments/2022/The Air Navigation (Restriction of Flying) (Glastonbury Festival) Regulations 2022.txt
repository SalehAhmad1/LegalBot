The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3 by reason of the large gathering of people due to attend the Glastonbury 
Festival 2022. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Glastonbury 
Festival) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 19th June 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0600 hours on 19th June 2022 and 1600 hours on 28 
June 2022 no aircraft is to fly below 3,100 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a 
circle having a radius of 2.5 nautical miles whose centre is at 510921N 0023510W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operating by or on behalf of the— 
(i) National Police Air Service, 
(ii) Helicopter Emergency Medical Services, or 
(iii) Maritime and Coastguard Agency, 
(b) flying with the permission of the— 
(i) Chief Officer of Police for Avon and Somerset, or 
(ii) Avon and Somerset Police Drone Unit Operations Manager, or 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations. 
(b) Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Glastonbury, Somerset by reason 
of the Glastonbury Festival 2022 that is scheduled to take place there on the 10 days between 19th 
June 2022 and 28th June 2022 (inclusive). A large gathering of people will attend this event on 
each day and therefore the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that 
flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the Chief Constable of Avon and 
Somerset Constabulary may be contacted on telephone number 01278 646320 and the Avon and
