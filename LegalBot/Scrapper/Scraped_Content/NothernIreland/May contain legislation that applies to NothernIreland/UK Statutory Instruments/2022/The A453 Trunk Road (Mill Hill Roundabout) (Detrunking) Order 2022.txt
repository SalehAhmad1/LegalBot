HIGHWAYS, ENGLAND
The A453 Trunk Road (Mill Hill Roundabout) (Detrunking) Order 2022
Made
7th December 2022
Coming into force
3rd January 2023
The Secretary of State for Transport makes this Order in exercise of powers conferred by sections 10(2) and 12(3) of the Highways Act 1980(1) and now vested in the Secretary of State(2).
In accordance with section 10(2) of that Act, the Secretary of State has taken into consideration the requirements of local and national planning, including the requirements of agriculture, and is satisfied as to expediency.
This Order is made in compliance with the provisions of Part I and III of Schedule 1 of that Act.

7th December 2022Regulation 3
SCHEDULE
AREA OF THE TRUNK ROAD CEASING TO BE A TRUNK ROAD
The area of the trunk road ceasing to be a trunk road is 360 square metres of the existing A453 Trunk Road forming part of the Mill Hill Roundabout, in the District of Rushcliffe, in the County of Nottinghamshire, as show by cross hatching on the plan.EXPLANATORY NOTE
(This note is not part of the Order)
The Mill Hill Roundabout on the A453 is being reconfigured to include a new junction and roundabout arrangement connecting onto a classified road which leads on to development to the east. The purpose of the Order is to remove the trunk road status from the length of the trunk road in the Schedule to the Order, the responsibility for which will pass to the local highway authority, Nottinghamshire County Council.
