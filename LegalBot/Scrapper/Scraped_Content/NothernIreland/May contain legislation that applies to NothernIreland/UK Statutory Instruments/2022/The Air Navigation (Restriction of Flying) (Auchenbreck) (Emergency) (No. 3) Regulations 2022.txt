The Secretary of State for a reason affecting the public interest has decided that it is necessary to 
restrict flying in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of an emergency having arisen in 
that area. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Auchenbreck) 
(Emergency) (No. 3) Regulations 2022 and they come into force with immediate effect. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1000 hours on 24th May 2022 and 1700 hours on 31st 
May 2022 no aircraft is to fly below 3,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a 
circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles whose centre is at 555913N 0050951W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operating by or on behalf of— 
(i) Police Scotland, 
(ii) the Scottish Ambulance Service, or 
(iii) the Maritime and Coastguard Agency for the purposes of search and rescue only, or 
(b) flying in accordance with a permission granted by a Police Scotland Aviation Safety and 
Security Officer. 
 
 
 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations. 
(b) Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Auchenbreck, Scotland. They 
are of temporary duration to accommodate the emergency in question. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), a Police Scotland Aviation Safety 
and Security Officer may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
