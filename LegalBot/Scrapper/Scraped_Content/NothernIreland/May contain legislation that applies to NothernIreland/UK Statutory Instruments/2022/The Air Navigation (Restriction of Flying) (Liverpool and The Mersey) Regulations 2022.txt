The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict unmanned flying in the 
vicinity of the area specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended arrival of the aircraft carrier 
HMS Queen Elizabeth. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Liverpool and 
The Mersey) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 24th March 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on unmanned flying in the vicinity of Liverpool and The 
Mersey by reason of the intended visit of the aircraft carrier HMS Queen Elizabeth which is 
scheduled to take place there between 24th March 2022 and 28th March 2022 (inclusive). The 
Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned 
aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the ship for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) up to 02:00 hours on 27th March 2022 after which it is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), Merseyside Police may be contacted as 
notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
