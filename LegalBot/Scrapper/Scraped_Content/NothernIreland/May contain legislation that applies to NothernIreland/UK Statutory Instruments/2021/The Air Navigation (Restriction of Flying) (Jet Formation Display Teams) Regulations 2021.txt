The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
the places specified in column 1 of the table in the Schedule by reason of the intended holding of 
flying displays there by a jet formation display team. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Jet Formation 
Display Teams) Regulations 2021 and come into force on 4th June 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), during the period commencing at 0800 hours and ending at 
2000 hours at each of the places specified in column 1 and on the corresponding date or dates 
specified in column 4 of the table in the Schedule no aircraft is to fly— 
(a) in any of the airspace above the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 6 nautical 
miles whose centre is at the point specified in column 2 of the table, and 
(b) below the corresponding altitude specified in column 3 of the table. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) flying within controlled airspace or within an aerodrome traffic zone in respect of which 
there is an appropriate air traffic control unit(c) if it flies in accordance with a clearance 
or permission of the unit controlling that airspace or zone, or 
(b) flying in accordance with a permission issued by the relevant flying display director. 
 
 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations. 
(b) Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
(c) “Appropriate air traffic control unit” is defined in Schedule 1 to S.I. 2016/765.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in airspace in various locations and airspace as 
specified in the Schedule during flying displays by jet formation teams such as the Royal Air 
Force Aerobatic Team, the Red Arrows, which are due to take place during the period from 4th 
June 21 to 12th September 2021(inclusive). 
In order to protect the display aircraft, other air traffic and spectators, it is considered that flying 
should be restricted in the vicinity of the airspace within which the display teams will perform. 
This has been agreed with the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department of Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the flying display director may be 
contacted on a telephone number which will be notified by Notice to Airmen (NOTAM). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and NOTAM. 
The point of contact in the Civil Aviation Authority is Mr S J Maxted, Airspace Regulator, Safety 
and Airspace Regulation Group, on telephone number 03301 383216.
