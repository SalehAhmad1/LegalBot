The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the vicinity of Birmingham City Centre by reason of the intended holding of 
Birmingham Pride. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Birmingham) 
Regulations 2021 and come into force on 25th September 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Birmingham City Centre during Birmingham Pride that is scheduled to take place there between 
25th and 27th September 2021 (inclusive). In view of the high visibility of this event and at the 
request of the West Midland Police Service, the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for 
Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted for reasons of safety and 
security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), Inspector Colwell of the West Midlands 
Police Service may be contacted on telephone number 07387 097782. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
