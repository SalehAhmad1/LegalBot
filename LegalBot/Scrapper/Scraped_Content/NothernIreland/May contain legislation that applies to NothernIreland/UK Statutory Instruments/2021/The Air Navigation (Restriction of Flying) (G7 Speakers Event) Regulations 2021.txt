The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the vicinity of Chorley and Preston, Lancashire by reason of the intended holding of the 
G7 Speakers Event which is scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (G7 Speakers 
Event) Regulations 2021 and they come into force on 16th September 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Chorley and Preston, Lancashire during the G7 Speakers Event that is scheduled to take place 
there between 16th and 19th September 2021. In view of the high visibility of this event and at the 
request of the Lancashire Police Service, the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for 
Transport agree that unmanned aircraft flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for 
reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Lancashire Police Service may be 
contacted on telephone number 0306 770 7750. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
