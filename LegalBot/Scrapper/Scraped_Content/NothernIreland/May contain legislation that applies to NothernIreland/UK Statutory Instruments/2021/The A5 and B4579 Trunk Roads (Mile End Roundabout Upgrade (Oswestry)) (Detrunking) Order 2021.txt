HIGHWAYS, ENGLAND
The A5 and B4579 Trunk Roads (Mile End Roundabout Upgrade (Oswestry)) (Detrunking) Order 2021
Made
21st October 2021
Coming into force
4th November 2021
The Secretary of State for Transport makes this Order in exercise of powers conferred by sections 10 and 12 of the Highways Act 1980(1) and now vested in him(2):-
In accordance with section 10(2) of that Act, the Secretary of State has taken into consideration the requirements of local and national planning, including the requirements of agriculture, and is satisfied as to the expediency.
This Order is made in compliance with the provisions of Parts I and III of Schedule 1 of that Act.

THE SCHEDULE
LENGTHS OF TRUNK ROADS CEASING TO BE TRUNK ROADS
1. A length of the B4579 from its junction with the Mile End Roundabout, given reference letter F on the plan, eastwards for a distance 234 metres, given the reference letter E on the plan; and
2. A length of the A5 from its junction with the Mile End Roundabout, given reference letter I on the plan, northwards for a distance of 377 metres, given the reference letter J on the plan;
and shown by woven hatching on the plan.EXPLANATORY NOTE
(This note is not part of the Order)
The Order removes the trunk road status from lengths of the A5 North and B4579 near Mile End Roundabout in the County of Shropshire and makes them classified roads. The transfer in classification and responsibility for the classified roads takes place on the date on which National Highways notifies Shropshire Council that the new trunk roads are open to traffic.
