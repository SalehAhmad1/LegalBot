The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the vicinity of Glasgow Green, Glasgow by reason of the intended gathering of a large 
number of persons at the TRNSMT Music Festival. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (TRNSMT 
Festival, Glasgow) Regulations 2021 and they come into force on 10th September 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Glasgow during the TRNSMT music festival that is scheduled to take place there between 10th 
and 12th September 2021 (inclusive). A large number of spectators will attend the festival and 
therefore Police Scotland considers that unmanned aircraft flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of Glasgow Green for reasons of public safety and security. The restrictions have been 
agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), Police Scotland may be contacted as 
notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
