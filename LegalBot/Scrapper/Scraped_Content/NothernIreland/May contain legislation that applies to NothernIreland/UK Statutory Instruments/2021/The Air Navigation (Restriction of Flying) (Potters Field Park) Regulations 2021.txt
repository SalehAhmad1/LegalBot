The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the vicinity of Potters Field Park, London, by reason of establishing a Fans Zone 
associated with the UEFA Euro 2020 football championship. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Potters Field 
Park) Regulations 2021 and come into force on 13th June 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), no unmanned aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea 
level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 0.5 nautical miles whose centre is at 
513015N 0004420W between— 
(a) 0900 hours and 1900 hours on 13th June 2021, 
(b) 1400 hours and 2259 hours on 18th June 2021, 
(c) 1400 hours and 2259 hours on 22nd June 2021, 
(d) 0900 hours on 26th June 2021 and 2259 hours on 29th June 2021 (inclusive), 
(e) 0900 hours and 2259 hours on 2nd and 3rd July 2021, 
(f) 0900 hours and 2259 hours on 6th and 7th July 2021, and 
(g) 0900 hours and 2259 hours on 11th July 2021. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any unmanned aircraft operating with the permission of the 
Metropolitan Police.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of Potters 
Field Park, London during football matches associated with the UEFA Euro 2020 football 
championship. A large number of spectators will attend the area and therefore the Metropolitan 
Police considers that unmanned aircraft flying should be restricted for reasons of public safety and 
security. This has been agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
