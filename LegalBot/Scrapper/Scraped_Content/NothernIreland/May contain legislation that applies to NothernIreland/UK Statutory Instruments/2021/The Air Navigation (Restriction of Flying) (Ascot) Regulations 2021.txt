The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the vicinity of Ascot Racecourse, Berkshire, by reason of the intended gathering of large 
numbers of persons associated with the Royal Ascot 2021 horse racing event. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Ascot) 
Regulations 2021 and come into force on 15th June 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of Ascot 
Racecourse, Berkshire, by reason of the gathering of large numbers of persons at the Royal Ascot 
2021 horse racing event on the five days between 15th and 19th June 2021(inclusive). The Civil 
Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft 
should be restricted for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC), which for the 
purpose of these Regulations is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the air traffic control unit at Ascot 
Heliport may be contacted as notified.   
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
