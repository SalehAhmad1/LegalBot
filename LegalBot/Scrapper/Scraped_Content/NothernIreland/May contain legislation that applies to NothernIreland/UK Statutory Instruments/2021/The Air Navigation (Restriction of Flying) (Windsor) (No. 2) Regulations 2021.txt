Coming into force in accordance with regulation 1(2) and (3) 
The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
Windsor, Berkshire, by reason of the funeral of His Royal Highness The Duke of Edinburgh. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation, commencement and interpretation 
1.—(1) These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Windsor) 
(No. 2) Regulations 2021. 
(2) These Regulations come into force at 0900 hours on 17th April 2021. 
(3) References to time in these Regulations are to Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
2.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0900 hours and 1600 hours on 17th April 2021 no 
aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded successively by— 
(a) a straight line joining the following points— 
(i) 513611N 0004133W, 
(ii) 513612N 0003203W, 
(b) a clockwise arc of a circle having a radius of 12 nautical miles centred on 512814N 
0004625W between 513612N 0003203W and 512013N 0003210W,  
(c) straight lines joining successively the following points— 
(i) 512013N 0003210W,

(iii) 512104N 0004242W, and 
(d) a clockwise arc of a circle having a radius of 12 nautical miles centred on 512812N 
0002713W between 512104N 0004242W and 513611N 0004133W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations restrict flying in the vicinity of Windsor on the occasion of the funeral of His 
Royal Highness The Duke of Edinburgh which is scheduled to take place on 17th April 2021. 
As part of the overall security arrangements and as advised by the Metropolitan Police Service, the 
Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that it is in the public interest to 
restrict flying in the area specified in these Regulations.  
The times mentioned in these Regulations are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one 
hour behind British Summer Time (BST). The restriction has effect from 0900 to 1600 hours Co-
ordinated Universal Time (which is the equivalent of 1000 to 1700 hours British Summer Time) 
on 17th April 2021.

In relation to regulation 2(2)(c)(i), the Denham Local Flying Area is the area comprising the 
portion of the aerodrome traffic zone contained within the London Control Zone defined in the 
United Kingdom Aeronautical Information Publication EGLD AD 2.22. 
In relation to regulation 2(2)(c)(ii), the Fairoaks Local Flying Area is the area comprising the 
aerodrome traffic zone defined in the United Kingdom Aeronautical Information Publication 
EGTF AD 2.22. 
In relation to regulation 2(2)(c)(iii), the White Waltham Local Flying Area is the area comprising 
the portion of the aerodrome traffic zone contained within the London Control Zone defined in the 
United Kingdom Aeronautical Information Publication EGLM AD 2.22. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 2(2)(e), the Metropolitan Police may be 
contacted on telephone number 07919293365. 
These Regulations also revoke the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Windsor) Regulations 
2021 (S.I. 2021/460), which have imposed restrictions on flying unmanned aircraft in the vicinity 
of Windsor since 9th April 2021.
