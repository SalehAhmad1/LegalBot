The Welsh Ministers, as traffic authority for the 
relevant lengths of the M4 London to South Wales 
Motorway and the A48(M) Motorway, are satisfied 
that traffic on specified lengths of the motorways 
should be prohibited because of works proposed on or 
near the motorways. 
The Welsh Ministers, therefore, in exercise of the 
powers conferred upon them by section 14(1) and (4) 
and 124(1)(d) of, and paragraph 27(1) of Schedule 9 
to, the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(1), make this 
Order.

2. In this Order: 
“exempted vehicle” (“cerbyd esempt”) means any 
vehicle being used by the emergency services or in 
connection with the works giving rise to this 
Order; 
“the motorways” (“y traffyrdd”) means the M4 
London to South Wales Motorway and the 
A48(M) Motorway;  
“works period” (“cyfnod y gwaith”) means 
intermittent periods commencing at 20:00 hours on 
3 January 2019 and ending when the temporary 
traffic signs for each period of works are removed. 
Prohibition 
3. No person may, during the works period, drive 
any vehicle, other than an exempted vehicle, on the 
lengths of the motorways specified in the Schedule to 
this Order. 
Application 
4. The prohibition in article 3 applies only during 
such times and to such extent as indicated by traffic 
signs. 
Revocation 
5. The M4 Motorway (Junction 23a (Magor), 
Monmouthshire to Junction 34 (Miskin), Rhondda 
Cynon Taf) & The A48(M) Motorway (Junction 29 
(Castleton), Newport to Junction 29A (St Mellons), 
Cardiff) (Temporary Prohibition of Vehicles) Order 
2018(2) is revoked. 
Duration of this Order 
6. The maximum duration for this Order is 18 
months.
