ROAD TRAFFIC, WALES 
The A487 & A40 Trunk Roads 
(Fishguard, Pembrokeshire) 
(Temporary Prohibition of Vehicles) 
Order 2018 
Made 
 20 December 2018 
Coming into force 
31 December 2018 
The Welsh Ministers, as traffic authority for the A487 
Trunk Road and the relevant length of the A40 Trunk 
Road, are satisfied that traffic on specified lengths of 
the trunk roads should be prohibited because of the 
New Year’s Eve street festival event being held on or 
near the roads. 
The Welsh Ministers, therefore, in exercise of the 
powers conferred upon them by section 16A of the 
Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(1), as amended by 
The Road Traffic Regulation (Special Events) Act 
1994, make this Order. 
Title, Commencement and Interpretation  
1. The title of this Order is the A487 & A40 Trunk 
Roads 
(Fishguard, 
Pembrokeshire) 
(Temporary 
Prohibition of Vehicles) Order 2018 and it comes into 
force on 31 December 2018. 
2. In this Order: 
“exempted vehicle” (“cerbyd esempt”) means: 
(i) any vehicle being used for police,  fire or 
ambulance services purposes; 
(ii) any vehicle being used in connection

“event period” (“cyfnod y digwyddiad”) means

“the trunk roads” (“y cefnffyrdd”) means the A487 
Fishguard to Bangor Trunk Road and the A40 
London to Fishguard Trunk Road. 
Prohibition 
3. No person may, during the event period, drive any 
vehicle, other than an exempted vehicle, on the lengths 
of the trunk roads specified in the Schedule to this 
Order. 
Application 
4. The prohibition in article 3 applies only during 
such times and to such extent as indicated by traffic 
signs. 
Duration of this Order 
5. The maximum duration of this Order is 2 days.
