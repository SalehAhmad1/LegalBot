The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of a Royal Aero Club Air Race 
that is scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Fenland 
Airfield) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 17th August 2024.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Fenland Airfield, Spalding, 
Lincolnshire, by the reason of the intended holding there of a Royal Aero Club Air Race which is 
scheduled to take place on 17th and 18th August 2024. 
A large number of aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the event and 
therefore the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should 
be restricted in the vicinity of the race for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3)(a)(i), the Fenland Airfield, Air Race air-
ground service may be contacted as notified. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3)(a)(ii), the Royal Aero Club Records, 
Racing and Rally Association Race Officials may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
