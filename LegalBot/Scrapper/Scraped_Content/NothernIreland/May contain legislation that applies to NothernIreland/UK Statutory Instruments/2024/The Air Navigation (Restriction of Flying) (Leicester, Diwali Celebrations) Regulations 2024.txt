The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of 
Diwali Celebrations there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Leicester, 
Diwali Celebrations) Regulations 2024, and they come into force on 18th October 2024. 
Interpretation 
2.—(1) In regulation 3(2) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (3) and during the periods specified in paragraph (2), no unmanned 
aircraft is to fly below 1,300 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 1 nautical mile whose centre is at 523857N 0010713W. 
(2) The periods referred to in paragraph (1) are between— 
(a) 1100 hours on 18th October 2024 and 0001 hours on 21st October 2024, and 
(b) 1200 hours on 31st October 2024 and 0100 hours on 2nd November 2024. 
(3) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any unmanned aircraft flying in accordance with a 
permission granted by Leicestershire Police.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in airspace in the 
vicinity of Leicester City Centre during the 2024 Diwali Celebrations that are scheduled to take 
place there from 18th to 21st October 2024, inclusive, and from 31st October and 2nd November 
2024, inclusive. 
Due to the large number of persons expected at this event and at the request of Leicestershire 
Police, the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be 
restricted for reasons of safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC), which for 
regulation 3(2)(a) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST) and for regulation 3(2)(b) is the 
same as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3), Leicestershire Police may be contacted 
as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
