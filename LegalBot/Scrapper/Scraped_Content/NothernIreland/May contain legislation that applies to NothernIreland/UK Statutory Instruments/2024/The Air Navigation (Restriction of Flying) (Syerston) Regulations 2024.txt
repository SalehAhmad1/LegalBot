The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of flying displays that are 
scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Syerston) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 21st August 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1030 hours and 1630 hours on 21st August 2024 no 
aircraft is to fly below 8,300 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 4 nautical miles whose centre is at 530120N 0005458W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operating in accordance and with the permission of the— 
(i) Royal Air Force Air Cadet National Air and Space Camp flying display director, or 
(ii) Royal Air Force Air Cadet National Air and Space Camp deputy flying display 
director, or 
(b) operated by or on behalf of the— 
(i) National Police Air Service, or 
(ii) Helicopter Emergency Medical Services.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Royal Air Force Syerston, 
Nottinghamshire, during flying displays that are scheduled to take place there for the Air Cadet 
National Air and Space Camp on 21st August 2024. 
Many aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the displays and therefore the 
Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in 
the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 3(2)(a), the Royal Air Force Air Cadet 
National Air and Space Camp flying display director and the deputy flying display director may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
