The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the holding there of the Trooping of the Colour 
ceremony and associated rehearsals for the event. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Trooping of the 
Colour) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 1st June 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0730 hours and 1200 hours on 1st and 8th June 2024 
and between 0730 hours and 1300 hours on 15th June 2024 no aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet 
above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles 
centred at 513000N 0000801W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying above Central London for the rehearsals of the 
Trooping of the Colour ceremony which are due to take place there on 1st and 8th June 2024 and 
for the actual ceremony on 15th June 2024. 
The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be 
restricted in the vicinity of the Trooping of the Colour rehearsals and the ceremony for reasons of 
public safety and security. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (City of London) Regulations 2004 (S.I. 2004/2092), the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Specified Area) Regulations 2005 (S.I. 2005/964), and the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Hyde Park) Regulations 2017 (S.I. 2017/1300). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b)(iii), the Metropolitan Police Service 
may be contacted on telephone number 07956 968118 or via email dean.akinola@met.police.uk.
