The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there 
of the Labour Party Conference. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Liverpool) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 20th September 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 2200 hours on 20th September 2024 and 0500 hours 
on 26th September 2024 no unmanned aircraft is to fly below 2,000 feet above mean sea level 
within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 0.9 of a nautical mile whose centre is at 
532347N 0025923W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any unmanned aircraft— 
(a) flying in accordance with a permission issued by the Merseyside Police Airspace Co-
ordinator, or 
(b) operated by or on behalf of the Emergency Services in response to an emergency incident.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of the 
M&S Bank Arena, Kings Dock, Liverpool during the 2024 Labour Party Conference that is due to 
take place from 20th to 26th September 2024, inclusive. 
The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned 
aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event location for reasons of public safety and 
security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(a), the Merseyside Police Airspace Co-
ordinator may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
