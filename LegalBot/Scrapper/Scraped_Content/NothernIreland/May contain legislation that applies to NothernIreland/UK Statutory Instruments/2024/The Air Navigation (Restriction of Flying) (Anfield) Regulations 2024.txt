The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intending gatherings of 
large numbers of persons in the vicinity of Anfield Stadium, Liverpool. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Anfield) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 24th June 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of Anfield 
Stadium, Liverpool where gatherings of large numbers of persons are due to take place for music 
concerts on 24th and 25th June 2024. 
At the request of the Merseyside Police Service, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department 
for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the 
concert for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Merseyside Police Service may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
