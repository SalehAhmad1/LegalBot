The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended holding there of flying displays associated 
with the Royal International Air Tattoo 2024. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Royal 
International Air Tattoo, Royal Air Force Fairford) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 
17th July 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3 the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Royal Air Force Fairford, 
Gloucestershire during the Royal International Air Tattoo 2024 which is due to take place there 
between 17th July 2024 and 22nd July 2024 (inclusive). 
Many aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the event and therefore the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of Royal Air Force Fairford for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(3) and 3(4) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is 
one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
