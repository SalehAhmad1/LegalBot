1.  These Regulations may be cited as the Licensing (Sample) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2022 and shall come into operation on 7th April 2022.Citation, commencement and interpretation
2.  In these Regulations “the 1996 Order” means the Licensing (Northern Ireland) Order 1996.Application
3.  These Regulations apply to a local producer’s licence in accordance with Articles 52B and 52D of the 1996 Order.Sample
4.  A sample of intoxicating liquor, is an amount (whether in one serving or more) which does not exceed the amount set out in the Schedule.Sealed with the Official Seal of the Department for Communities on 2nd March 2022.

SCHEDULE
Intoxicating Liquor Amount
(*)
ABV = Alcohol by volume
Beer – up to and including 6% ABV(*) 852 mls
Beer – above 6% ABV 568 mls
Cider – up to and including 6% ABV 852 mls
Cider – above 6% ABV 568 mls
Spirits 105 mls
Liqueur 210 mlsEXPLANATORY NOTE
(This note is not part of the Regulations)
These Regulations prescribe the amount of intoxicating liquor which may be provided as a sample for consumption on a tour given of production premises under Article 52B of the Licensing (NI) Order 1996 (“the 1996 Order”) or at an event not on licensed premises under Article 52D of the 1996 Order. The amount (whether in one serving or more) must not exceed:
(a)
852 mls beer or cider up to and including 6% ABV;
(b)
568 mls beer or cider above 6% ABV;
(c)
105 mls of any spirit;
(d)
210 mls of any liqueur.
