The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Article 4(1), (2) and (3) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 
1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
Article 4(6) and paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 1 to that Order. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
No written objection or other representation has been received. 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as the Prohibition of U-Turn (Ballygawley) Order (Northern Ireland) 
2024 and shall come into operation on 24th June 2024. 
Prohibition of U-Turn 
2. Subject to Article 3 a person shall not cause or permit any vehicle proceeding in either 
direction along Omagh Road, Ballygawley, to make a U-Turn on that road, from its junction with 
Ballygawley Roundabout to a point 350 metres north-west of that junction. 
Exceptions 
3. Article 2 shall not apply to— 
(a) a vehicle being used for fire and rescue, ambulance, police or customs purposes or for the 
purposes of the security forces; or 
(b) a vehicle, the driver or rider of which is acting upon the direction, or with the permission, 
of a constable in uniform.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order prohibits vehicles proceeding in either direction along Omagh Road, Ballygawley from 
making a U-Turn on the length of road specified in Article 2. 
Vehicles are excepted from the prohibition in certain circumstances (Article 3). 
Any person who acts in contravention of this Order shall be guilty of an offence under the Road 
Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 1997 (“the Regulation Order”) and shall be liable on 
summary conviction to a fine not exceeding level 3 on the standard scale (£1,000), in accordance 
with the Road Traffic Offenders (Northern Ireland) Order 1996 as amended by the Regulation 
Order. 
Traffic signs indicating the effect of this Order will in due course be placed on the road.
