The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Articles 37(3), 37(4) and 38(1) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) 
Order 1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 5 to that Order. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
No objection or other representation has been received. 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as the Roads (Speed Limit) (No. 4) Order (Northern Ireland) 2023 
and shall come into operation on 11th December 2023. 
Application of speed limits 
2. The driving of motor vehicles at a speed exceeding 40 miles per hour on the length of road 
specified in Schedule 1 is prohibited. 
De-restriction of speed on certain roads 
3. The Department directs that the lengths of road specified in Schedule 2 shall not be restricted 
roads notwithstanding that a system of street lighting furnished by lamps placed not more than 185 
metres apart is provided on those lengths of road. 
Revocations 
4. The provisions described in Schedule 3 are revoked.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order— 
(a) applies a speed limit of 40 miles per hour to the length of road specified in Schedule 1; 
(b) makes the lengths of road specified in Schedule 2 de-restricted roads; and 
(c) revokes the provisions in Schedule 3. 
In relation to the revoked provisions in Schedule 3— 
1. The speed limit on the length of Moneynick Road, Randalstown (item 114 of paragraph 1 of 
Schedule 3) is now set at the national speed limit (60 miles per hour). 
2. The speed limit on the length of Hillhead Road, Toomebridge (item 127 of paragraph 1 of 
Schedule 3) is now set at 40 miles per hour. 
3. The speed limit on the length of Hillhead Road, Toomebridge (paragraph 2 of Schedule 3) is 
now set at 40 miles per hour.
