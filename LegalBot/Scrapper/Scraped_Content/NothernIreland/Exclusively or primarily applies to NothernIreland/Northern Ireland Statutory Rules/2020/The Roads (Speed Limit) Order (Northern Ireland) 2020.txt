The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Articles 37(3), 37(4) and 38(1)(a) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) 
Order 1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 5 to that Order. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
One written objection was received and subsequently withdrawn and no other representation has 
been received. 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as The Roads (Speed Limit) Order (Northern Ireland) 2020 and shall 
come into operation on 7th September 2020. 
Speed restriction on certain roads 
2. The Department directs that the length of road specified in Schedule 1 is a restricted road. 
Application of speed limits 
3. The driving of motor vehicles at a speed exceeding 40 miles per hour on the road and lengths 
of road specified in Schedule 2 is prohibited. 
4. The driving of motor vehicles at a speed exceeding 50 miles per hour on the length of road 
specified in Schedule 3 is prohibited.

De-restriction of speed on certain roads 
5. The Department directs that the length of road specified in Schedule 4 shall not be a restricted 
road notwithstanding that a system of street lighting furnished by means of lamps placed not more 
than 185 metres apart is provided on that length of road. 
Revocations 
6. The provisions described in Schedule 5 are revoked.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order— 
(a) makes the length of road specified in Schedule 1 a restricted road; 
(b) applies a speed limit of 40 miles per hour to the road and lengths of road specified in 
Schedule 2; 
(c) applies a speed limit of 50 miles per hour to the length of road specified in Schedule 3; 
(d) makes the length of road specified in Schedule 4 a de-restricted road; and 
(e) revokes the provisions in Schedule 5. 
In relation to the revoked provisions in Schedule 5— 
1. The speed limit on the lengths of Gulladuff Road, Bellaghy (paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 
5) is now partly set at 30 miles per hour (restricted by street lighting) and now partly the national 
speed limit (60 miles per hour). 
2. The speed limit on the length of Maydown Road, Londonderry (paragraph 4 of Schedule 5) is 
now a longer length of road set at 40 miles per hour. 
3. The speed limit on the length of Woodgreen Road, Kells (paragraph 3 of Schedule 5) is now 
included in a longer length of road set at 40 miles per hour. 
4. The speed limit on the lengths of Derrymacash Road and Derrytrasna Road, Lurgan 
(paragraph 5 and items 160 and 161 of paragraph 6 of Schedule 5) are now 30 miles per hour 
(restricted by street lighting). 
5. The speed limit on the length of Moneymore Road, Loughbrickland (item 281 of paragraph 6 
of Schedule 5) is now 30 miles per hour (restricted by street lighting). 
6. The speed limit on the length of Armagh Road, Newry (paragraph 7 of Schedule 5) is now 
included in a longer length of road at 30 miles per hour (restricted by street lighting). 
7. The speed limit on the length of Dublin Road, Omagh (paragraph 8 of Schedule 5) is now 
included in a longer length of road set at 40 miles per hour. 
8. The speed limit on the Drumbroughas North Road, Lisnaskea (paragraph 9 of Schedule 5) is 
now 30 miles per hour (restricted by street lighting). 
9. The speed limit on the lengths of Lisnagole Road, Lisnaskea (paragraph 10 of Schedule 5) is 
now partly set at 40 miles per hour and partly set at 50 miles per hour. 
10. The speed limit on the length of Moneymore Road, Magherafelt (paragraph 11 of Schedule 
5) is now set at 40 miles per hour.
