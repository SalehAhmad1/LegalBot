The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Article 4(1), (2) and (3) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 
1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 1 to that Order. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
No written objection or other representations have been received. 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as The Prohibition of U-Turns (Belfast) Order (Northern Ireland) 
2021 and shall come into operation on 25th October 2021. 
Prohibition of U-Turns 
2. Subject to Article 3 a person shall not cause or permit any vehicle proceeding on the roads 
specified in Column 1 of Schedule 1 to make a U-Turn where indicated in relation to the said 
roads in Column 2 while proceeding in the direction specified in Column 3. 
Exceptions 
3. The prohibition in Article 2 shall not apply to— 
(a) a vehicle being used for fire and rescue, ambulance, police or customs purposes or for the 
purposes of the security forces; or 
(b) a vehicle, the driver or rider of which is acting upon the direction, or with the permission, 
of a constable in uniform.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order prohibits vehicles proceeding in a south-westerly direction along Bridge Street, Belfast 
from making a U-Turn at its junction with High Street specified in Schedule 1.  
Vehicles are excepted from the prohibition in certain circumstances (Article 3). 
The Order also revokes a number of Statutory Rules to the extent specified in Schedule 2 (Article 
4). The prohibition of U-Turns at Albert Bridge, Albertbridge Road, Andersonstown Road, East 
Bridge Street, Kennedy Way, Knock Road, Sydneham By-Pass, Upper Knockbreda Road, and 
University Road, and the prohibition of a right-hand turn along East Bridge Street in the revoked 
Statutory Rules are re-enacted to the extent specified in Schedule 1.  
The Order also updates references to the class of vehicles which are excepted from the prohibition. 
Any person who acts in contravention of this Order shall be guilty of an offence under the Road 
Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 1997 (“the Regulation Order”) and shall be liable on 
summary conviction to a fine not exceeding level 3 on the standard scale (£1,000), in accordance 
with the Road Traffic Offenders (Northern Ireland) Order 1996 as amended by the Regulation 
Order. 
Traffic signs indicating the effect of the Order will in due course be placed on the road.
