The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intending gathering of a 
large number of persons at music concerts due to take place at Murrayfield Stadium. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Murrayfield 
Stadium) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 7th June 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Murrayfield Stadium, Edinburgh during music concerts that are due to take place there between 
7th June 2024 and 9th June 2024 (inclusive). 
At the request of Police Scotland, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport 
agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the stadium for 
reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), Police Scotland may be contacted as 
notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
