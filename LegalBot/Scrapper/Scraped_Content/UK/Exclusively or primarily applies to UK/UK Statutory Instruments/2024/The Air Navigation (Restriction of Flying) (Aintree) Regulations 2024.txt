The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended gathering there 
of a large number of persons in the vicinity of Aintree Racecourse, Merseyside. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Aintree) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 11th April 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0700 hours and 1800 hours on each of the three days 
beginning on 11th April 2024 and ending on 13th April 2024 no unmanned aircraft is to fly below 
2,000 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 1 nautical 
mile centred at 532832N 0025657W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any unmanned aircraft— 
(a) operated by the Police UAS Unit or flying in accordance with a permission issued by that 
Unit, or 
(b) insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) 
(Prisons and Young Offender Institutions) (England and Wales) Regulations 2023(c).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of Aintree 
Racecourse, Merseyside by the reason of the large number of persons expected to attend the 2024 
Grand National event which is due to take place there between 11th April 2024 and 13th April 
2024 (inclusive). 
The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned 
aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event location for reasons of public safety. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Prisons and Young Offender Institutions) (England and Wales) 
Regulations 2023 (S.I. 2023/1101). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(a), the Police UAS Unit may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by an Aeronautical Information 
Service Briefing Sheet and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
