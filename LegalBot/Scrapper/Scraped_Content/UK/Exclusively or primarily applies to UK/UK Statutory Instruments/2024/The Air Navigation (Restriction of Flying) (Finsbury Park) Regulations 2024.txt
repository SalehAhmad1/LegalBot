The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended gathering there 
of a large number of persons in the vicinity of Finsbury Park, London. 
The Secretary of State makes these Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 
of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Finsbury Park) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 5th July 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1000 hours on 5th July 2024 and 2130 hours on 7th 
July 2024 no unmanned aircraft is to fly below 700 feet above mean sea level within the area 
bounded by straight lines joining successively— 
(a) 513426N 0000617W, 
(b) 513432N 0000555W, 
(c) 513415N 0000546W, 
(d) 513352N 0000619W, and 
(e) 513426N 0000617W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to an aircraft any unmanned aircraft flying in accordance with a 
permission issued by the event Airspace Co-ordinator.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Finsbury Park, London by reason of the large number of persons expected to attend a music 
festival which is due to take place there between 5th July 2024 and 7th July 2024 (inclusive). 
The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned 
aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event location for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the event Airspace Co-ordinator may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
