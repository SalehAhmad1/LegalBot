The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of flying displays. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Eastbourne) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 15th August 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1100 hours and 1700 hours on each of the four days 
beginning on 15th August 2024 and ending on 18th August 2024 no aircraft is to fly below 5,500 
feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by straight lines joining successively the 
following points—

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Eastbourne, East Sussex during 
flying displays associated with the Eastbourne International Airshow that are due to take place 
there from 15th August 2024 to 18th August 2024 (inclusive). 
Many aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the displays and therefore the 
Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(a), the Eastbourne International Air 
Show flying display director may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
