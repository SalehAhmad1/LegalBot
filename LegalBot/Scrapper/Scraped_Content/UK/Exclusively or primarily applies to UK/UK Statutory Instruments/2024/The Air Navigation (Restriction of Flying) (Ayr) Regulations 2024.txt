The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of flying displays at the 
2024 Ayr Show, Festival of Flight. 
The Secretary of State makes these Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 
of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Ayr) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 6th September 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1445 hours and 1930 hours on 6th September 2024 
and between 1200 hours and 1730 hours on 7th September 2024, no aircraft is to fly below 6,000 
feet above mean sea level within the area of a circle having a radius of 4 nautical miles whose 
centre is at 552720N 0043840W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of— 
(i) the Scottish Air Ambulance Service, 
(ii) the Maritime and Coastguard Agency whilst conducting Search and Rescue 
operations, or

control of the air traffic control unit at Glasgow Prestwick Airport, or

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Ayr, South Ayrshire by reason 
of the intended holding of the 2024 Ayr Show, Festival of Flight which is scheduled to take place 
there on 6th and 7th September 2024. 
A large number of persons will attend the event and therefore the Civil Aviation Authority and the 
Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the Ayr Show for 
reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(c), the Ayr Show, Festival of Flight, 
Flying Display Director may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
