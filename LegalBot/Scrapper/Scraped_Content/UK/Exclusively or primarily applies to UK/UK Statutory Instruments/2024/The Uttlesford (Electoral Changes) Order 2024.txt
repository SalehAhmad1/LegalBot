2024 No. 597
LOCAL GOVERNMENT, ENGLAND
The Uttlesford (Electoral Changes) Order 2024
Made
2nd May 2024
Coming into force in accordance with article 1(2), (3) and (4)
Under section 92(2) of the Local Government and Public Involvement in Health Act 2007(1), Uttlesford District Council (“the Council”) made recommendations in a review to the Local Government Boundary Commission for England(2) for the related alteration of the boundaries between two county electoral divisions and between two district wards within the Council’s area.
The Local Government Boundary Commission for England has decided to give effect to the recommendations and makes the following Order in exercise of the power conferred by section 92(3) of that Act.

2nd May 2024EXPLANATORY NOTE
(This note is not part of the Order)
This Order gives effect to recommendations made by Uttlesford District Council (“the Council”) to the Local Government Boundary Commission for England for related alterations to the boundaries between two county electoral divisions and between two district wards within the Council’s area.
The alterations are a consequence of changes to parish boundaries and warding arrangements under the District of Uttlesford (Reorganisation of Community Governance) Order 2023, which was made by the Council under powers including section 86 of the Local Government and Public Involvement in Health Act 2007 (c. 28). A hard copy of that Order is available for inspection at reasonable times at the offices of Uttlesford District Council, Council Offices, London Road, Saffron Walden CB11 4ER.
The changes described in article 2 (in relation to county electoral divisions) apply in respect of elections to be held on and after the ordinary day of election of councillors in England in 2025. The changes described in article 3 (in relation to district wards) apply in respect of elections to be held on and after the ordinary day of election of councillors in England in 2027.
A full impact assessment has not been produced for this instrument as no impact on the private, voluntary or public sectors is foreseen.
