The Secretary of State has previously decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict 
flying in the area of Aberdeenshire and Edinburgh by reason of two helicopters flights and now 
considers it necessary in the public interest to amend the specific timings on one flight and to add 
an exemption. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Helicopter 
Flight) (No. 6) (Amendment) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 3rd October 2022. 
Amendment 
2.—(1) The Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Helicopter Flight) (No. 6) Regulations 
2022(b) are amended as follows. 
(2) In regulation 3— 
(a) in subparagraph (2) delete “1425 hours and 1540 hours” and insert “1340 hours and 1455 
hours”, 
(b) in subparagraph (4)(a)(iii) omit “or”, and 
(c) in subparagraph (4)(a) insert “(v) Perth Airport whilst under the direction of the air traffic 
control unit at Perth Airport, or”.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations amend the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Helicopter Flight) (No. 6) 
Regulations 2022 (S.I. 2022/1008). The amendment amends the timings of one of the flights and 
adds an additional air traffic control unit (Perth Airport) from which a permission to enter into the 
restricted airspace may be obtained. 
Full details of these Regulations will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
