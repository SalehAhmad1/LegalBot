The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended holding of flying displays at Duxford 
Aerodrome. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Duxford) 
Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 17th June 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(2) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (3), during the periods specified in paragraph (2) no aircraft is to 
fly below 4,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 
2.2 nautical miles whose centre is at 520539N 0000753E. 
(2) The periods referred to in paragraph (1) are— 
(a) between 0800 hours and 1700 hours on 17th June 2022, 
(b) between 1100 hours and 1700 hours on 18th June 2022 and 19th June 2022, 
(c) between 0800 hours and 1700 hours on 9th September 2022, and 
(d) between 1100 hours and 1700 hours on 10th September 2022 and 11th September 2022. 
(3) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying in accordance with a permission issued by 
the Duxford Aerodrome flying display director.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Duxford Aerodrome, 
Cambridgeshire during air displays that are scheduled to take place there on 17th June 2022, 18th 
June 2022, 19th June 2022, 9th September 2022, 10th September 2022 and 11th September 2022. 
A large number of aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the displays and 
therefore the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be 
restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3), the Duxford Aerodrome flying display 
director may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
