The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended gathering of large numbers of persons 
associated with the Royal Ascot 2022 horse racing event. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Ascot) 
Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 14th June 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0900 hours and 1900 hours on each day beginning on 
14th June and ending on 18th June 2022 no aircraft is to fly below 2,000 feet above mean sea level 
within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles whose centre is at 512515N 
0003934W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying in accordance with a permission issued 
by— 
(a) the air traffic control unit at Ascot Heliport, or 
(b) the air traffic control unit at London Terminal Control, Swanwick.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Ascot Racecourse by reason of 
the gathering of large numbers of persons at the Royal Ascot 2022 horse racing event on the five 
days between 14th and 18th June 2022 (inclusive). The Civil Aviation Authority and Department 
for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public 
safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 3(2), the air traffic control units at Ascot 
Racecourse and the London Terminal Control, Swanwick may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
