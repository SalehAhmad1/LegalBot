The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of flying 
displays associated with the Flying Legends Air Show 2023. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Flying 
Legends, Leeds East) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 15th July 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Leeds East Aerodrome during 
air displays associated with the Flying Legends Air Show 2023 that are scheduled to take place 
there on 15th July 2023 and 16th July 2023. 
In order to protect the display aircraft, other air traffic and spectators, the Civil Aviation Authority 
and the Department for Transport agree that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Leeds East Air Show flying display 
director may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
