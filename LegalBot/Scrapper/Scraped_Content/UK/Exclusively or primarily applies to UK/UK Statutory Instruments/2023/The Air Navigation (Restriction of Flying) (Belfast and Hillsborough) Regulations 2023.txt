The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of security operations 
associated with events marking the 25th anniversary of the Good Friday agreement. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Belfast and 
Hillsborough) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 16th April 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between— 
(a) 1400 hours on 16th April 2023 and 2200 hours on 16th April 2023 and between 1400 
hours on 19th April 2023 and 2230 hours on 19th April 2023 no unmanned aircraft is to 
fly below 1,600 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a 
radius of 5 nautical miles centred at 542740N 0060505W, and 
(b) 0700 hours on 17th April 2023 and 1700 hours on 20th April 2023 no unmanned aircraft 
is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 5 nautical miles centred at 543544N 0055548W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any unmanned aircraft flying in accordance with a 
permission issued by the Police Service of Northern Ireland.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of Belfast 
and Hillsborough, Northern Ireland due to security operations associated with the events marking 
the 25th anniversary of the Good Friday agreement. 
As part of the overall security arrangements, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for 
Transport agree that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned aircraft in the 
vicinity of the events for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Police Service of Northern Ireland 
may be contacted on telephone number 07801 738257. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
