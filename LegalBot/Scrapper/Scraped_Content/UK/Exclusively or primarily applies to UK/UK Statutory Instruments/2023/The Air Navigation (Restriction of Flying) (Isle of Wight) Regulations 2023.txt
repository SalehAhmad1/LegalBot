The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended gathering there of a large number of 
persons associated with the Isle of Wight music festival. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Isle of Wight) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 14th June 2023.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of the Isle of Wight by reason of 
the intended gathering there of a large number of persons at the Isle of Wight music festival event 
on the six days between 14th June 2023 and 19th June 2023 (inclusive). 
The Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree it is in the public interest to 
restrict flying in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), Aero Fire & Rescue Limited at the 
Festival Heliport may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
