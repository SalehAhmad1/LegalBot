The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Topcliffe) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 3rd September 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0700 hours on 3rd September 2023 and 1600 hours on 
15th September 2023 no aircraft is to fly below 6,000 feet above mean sea level within the area 
bounded by a circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles centred at 541219N 0012329W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying in accordance with a permission issued by 
the— 
(a) Flight Control Officer, 2 Signal Squadron, 32 Signal Regiment, or 
(b) air traffic control unit at Royal Air Force Leeming.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Topcliffe, North Yorkshire 
because of the intended use there of an unlit and unmarked tethered balloon for a military exercise 
which is due to take place during the period 3rd September 2023 to 15th September 2023 
(inclusive). 
As the exercise does not allow the balloon or its tether to be lit or marked the Civil Aviation 
Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of 
Topcliffe for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Flight Control Officer, 2 Signal 
Squadron, 32 Signal Regiment may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
