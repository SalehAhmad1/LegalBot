STATUTORY INSTRUMENTS
2023 No. 917
ROAD TRAFFIC
The M48 Motorway (Severn Bridge Half Marathon) (Temporary Prohibition of Traffic) Order 2023
Made
18th August 2023
Coming into force
25th August 2023
The Secretary of State for Transport, being the traffic authority for relevant lengths of the M48 Motorway and connecting roads, is satisfied that traffic on lengths of that motorway in South Gloucestershire, the County of Gloucestershire and the County of Monmouthshire should be prohibited for the purpose of facilitating the holding of a relevant event.
The Secretary of State is further satisfied that it is not reasonably practicable for the event to be held otherwise than on a road.
The Secretary of State for Transport, in exercise of the powers conferred by section 16A(2)(a) of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(1), hereby makes the following Order—
(1)
1984 c. 27; Section 16A was inserted by the Road Traffic Regulation (Special Events) Act 1994 (c. 11), section 1(1). There are further amendments to the section that are not relevant to this Order.1.  This Order may be cited as the M48 Motorway (Severn Bridge Half Marathon) (Temporary Prohibition of Traffic) Order 2023 and shall come into force on 25th August 2023.2.  In this Order —
“the relevant lengths of the M48” means (i) the westbound carriageway of the M48 over the Severn Bridge from the tip of the nosing of the westbound exit slip road at Junction 1 to the tip of the nosing of the westbound entry slip road at Junction 2; and (ii) the eastbound carriageway of the M48 over the Severn Bridge from the tip of the nosing of the eastbound exit slip road at Junction 2 to the tip of the nosing of the eastbound entry slip road at Junction 1;
“the slip roads” mean (i) the eastbound entry slip road at M48 Junction 2; and (ii) the westbound entry slip road at M48 Junction 1;
“the footpath/cycle track” means the footpath/cycle track adjacent to the M48 eastbound carriageway between Junctions 2 and 1;
“the tip of the nosing” means the first point where an entry slip road joins the carriageway of a motorway or the last point where an exit slip road leaves the carriageway of a motorway;
“authorised traffic” means the following traffic classes, as specified in Schedule 4 to the Highways Act 1980(1): (a) Class VII – pedal cycles; (b) Class IX – pedestrians, perambulators, push-chairs and other forms of baby carriages and dogs on leads; (c) Class X – motor cycles where the engine cylinder size is less than 50 cubic centimetres; and (d) Class XI – invalid carriages;
“event” means the Severn Bridge Half Marathon; and
“the event period” means a period of 8 hours starting at 06.00 hours on any Sunday between Sunday 27th August 2023 and Monday 25th September 2023.

18th August 2023EXPLANATORY NOTE
(This note is not part of the Order)
This Order authorises the temporary prohibition of traffic on the M48 Motorway (Severn Bridge) on 27th August 2023 to facilitate the holding of the Severn Bridge Half Marathon.
