2023 No. 1101
CIVIL AVIATION
The Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Prisons and Young Offender Institutions) (England and Wales) Regulations 2023
Made
16th October 2023
Coming into force
25th January 2024
The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned aircraft in the areas specified in regulation 3(1) for reason of security and public safety considerations associated with the location there of prisons and young offender institutions.
The Secretary of State makes the following regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(1).
(1)
S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations.Citation and commencement and extent
1.  These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Prisons and Young Offender Institutions) (England and Wales) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 25th January 2024.Citation and commencement and extent
2.  These Regulations extend to England and Wales.Restricted Airspace
3.—(1) These Regulations apply to each of the facilities listed in Column 1 of the Schedule, each of which for the purpose of these Regulations comprise an area bounded by straight lines joining successively between each of the points in Column 2 of the Schedule.
(2) No unmanned flight shall fly over a facility to which these Regulations apply below the altitude specified in Column 3 of the Schedule.Permitted flight in restricted airspace
4.  Regulation 3 does not apply—
(a)
insofar as the flying of unmanned aircraft is already prohibited by the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Prisons) Regulations 2001(1), the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Specified Area) Regulations 2005(2),the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Scampton) (Restricted Zone EG R313) Regulations 2022(3), Flight Restriction Zone of London Heathrow Airport (Feltham and Bronzefield), London City Airport (Belmarsh, Thameside and Isis), Manchester Airport (Styal), London Heliport (Wandsworth and Brixton), Rochester Aerodrome (Rochester), RAF Tern Hill (Stoke Heath) and Norwich Airport (Norwich)(4),
(b)
to unmanned aircraft—
(i)
operated by or on behalf of—
(aa)
the Ministry of Justice,
(bb)
His Majesty’s Prison and Probation Service, or
(cc)
any police force or fire service of England and Wales in response to an emergency incident, or
(ii)
flying with the permission of the—
(aa)
National Incident Management Unit,
(bb)
His Majesty’s Prison and Probation Service, or
(cc)
individual or organisation responsible for managing a Flight Restricted Zone and/or Restricted Area.

(This note is not part of the Regulations)
These Regulations restrict the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of the prisons and young offender institutions in England and Wales as specified in the Schedule. It has been agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and the Ministry of Justice that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the areas specified in the Schedule for reasons of security and public safety.
These Regulations apply except insofar as the flying of an unmanned aircraft is already prohibited by the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Prisons) Regulations 2001 (S.I. 2001/1657), the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Specified Area) Regulations 2005 (S.I. 2005/964), the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Scampton) (Restricted Zone EG R313) Regulations 2022 (S.I. 2022/1221), Flight Restriction Zone (FRZ) of London Heathrow Airport (Feltham and Bronzefield), London City Airport (Belmarsh, Thameside and Isis), Manchester Airport (Styal), London Heliport (Wandsworth and Brixton), Rochester Aerodrome (Rochester), RAF Tern Hill (Stoke Heath), and Norwich Airport (Norwich).
In relation to permissions mentioned in regulation 4(b)(ii) the National Incident Management Unit, His Majesty’s Prison and Probation Service, and the Restricted Area and FRZ sponsors may be contacted as notified.
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated in the United Kingdom Aeronautical Information Publication.
In relation to this Statutory Instrument, His Majesty’s Prison and Probation Service may be contacted via drone.RFZapplication@justice.gov.uk.
