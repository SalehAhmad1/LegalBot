The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of events to commemorate 
the Coronation of His Majesty the King. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Coronation 
Events, London) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 5th May 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraphs (2) and (3), between 0800 hours on 5th May 2023 and 2000 hours 
on 6th May 2023 no aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area 
bounded by a circle having a radius of 2.15 nautical miles centred at 513029N 0000741W.

(a) operated by or on behalf of—

(iv) The King’s Helicopter Flight,

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying above Central London during the Coronation 
events which are scheduled to take place there on 5th May 2023 and 6th May 2023. The Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport have agreed that flying should be restricted 
in the vicinity of the Coronation events for reasons of public safety and security. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (City of London) Regulations 2004 (S.I. 2004/2092), the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Specified Area) Regulations 2005 (S.I. 2005/964), and the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Hyde Park) Regulations 2017 (S.I. 2017/1300). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(c), the Metropolitan Police may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
