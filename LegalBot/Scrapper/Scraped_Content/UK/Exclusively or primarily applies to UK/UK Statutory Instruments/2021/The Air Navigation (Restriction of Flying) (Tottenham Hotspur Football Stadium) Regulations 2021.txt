The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the vicinity of Tottenham Hotspur Football Stadium, London by reason of the intended 
gathering of a large number of persons at the NFL Football Games which are scheduled to take 
place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Tottenham 
Hotspur Football Stadium) Regulations 2021 and they come into force on 10th October 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Tottenham Hotspur Football Stadium, London during NFL Football Games that are scheduled to 
take place there on the 10th and 17th October 2021. A large number of spectators will attend the 
games and therefore the Metropolitan Police considers that unmanned aircraft flying should be 
restricted in the vicinity of Stadium for reasons of public safety and security. The restrictions have 
been agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Metropolitan Police may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
