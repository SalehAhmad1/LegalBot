STATUTORY INSTRUMENTS
2021 No. 491
EDUCATION, ENGLAND
The Power to Award Degrees etc. (TEC Partnership) (Amendment) Order 2021
Made
17th April 2021
Coming into force
1st August 2021
The Office for Students, in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 42(1) and 44(1) of the Higher Education and Research Act 2017(1), makes the following Order:
(1)
2017 c.29.Citation and commencement
1.  This Order may be cited as the Power to Award Degrees etc. (TEC Partnership) (Amendment) Order 2021 and shall come into force on 1st August 2021.Variation of Power to Award Degrees etc. (TEC Partnership) Order 2020
2.—(1) The Power to Award Degrees etc. (TEC Partnership) Order 2020 is varied in accordance with paragraphs (2) and (3).
(2) For paragraph 2, substitute—
“2.  TEC Partnership shall be competent to grant foundation degree only awards of the kind mentioned in section 42(2)(a) and defined under section 42(3) of the said Act for a fixed term beginning on 1st August 2020 and expiring on 31st October 2024.”
(3) After paragraph 2, insert the following entry—
“3.  TEC Partnership shall be competent to grant up to and including bachelor’s degree awards of the kind mentioned in section 42(2)(c) of the said Act for a fixed term beginning on 1st August 2021 and expiring on 31st October 2024.”Susan Lapworth
Director of Regulation
For the Office for Students
17th April 2021EXPLANATORY NOTE
(This note is not part of the Order)
This Order, made under sections 42(1) and 44(1) of the Higher Education and Research Act 2017, provides for a variation of the Power to Award Degrees etc. (TEC Partnership) Order 2020, so that TEC Partnership’s existing foundation Degree Awarding Powers authorisation remains valid until 31st October 2024. This Order also authorises TEC Partnership to grant awards up to and including bachelor’s degree awards for a fixed term expiring on 31st October 2024. This Order will come into force on 1st August 2021.
An impact assessment has not been produced for this instrument as it has no impact on businesses and civil society organisations. The instrument has no impact on the public sector.
