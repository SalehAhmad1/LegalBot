The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
Cornwall by reason of the G7 Summit that is scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Cornwall) 
Regulations 2021 and come into force on 6th June 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) and 4(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 2259 hours on 6th June 2021 and 2259 hours on 9th 
June 2021 no aircraft is to fly below 3,000 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a 
circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles whose centre is at 501152N 0052759W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying in accordance with a permission issued by 
the Devon and Cornwall Police Aviation Unit. 
4.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 2259 hours on 9th June 2021 and 2259 hours on 13th 
June 2021 no aircraft is to fly below Flight Level 100 within the area bounded by— 
(a) a clockwise arc of a circle, having a radius of 10 nautical miles whose centre is at 
501152N 0052759W between 500210N 0053003W and 501801N 0054024W, 
(b) a straight line between 501801N 0054024W and 503604N 0051753W, 
(c) a clockwise arc of a circle, having a radius of 15 nautical miles whose centre is at 
502627N 0045943W between 503604N 0051753W and 501141N 0050331W, and 
(d) a clockwise arc of a circle, having a radius of 10 nautical miles whose centre is at 
500507N 0051514W between 501141N 0050331W and 500210N 0053003W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Carbis Bay, St Ives (regulation 
3) and Cornwall (regulation 4) due to the G7 Summit that is scheduled to take place at Carbis Bay 
during the 4 days of 10th to 13th June 2021. 
In view of the high visibility of this event and the presence of many VIPs, Devon and Cornwall 
Police has requested that flying be restricted in the vicinity of the event for security and public 
safety reasons. This request has been agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department 
of Transport. 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 3(2) and 4(2)(c), the Devon and Cornwall 
Aviation Unit may be contacted as notified. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) and 4(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is 
one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
