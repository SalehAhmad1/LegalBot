ROAD TRAFFIC, WALES 
The A483 Trunk Road (West Street, 
Builth Wells, Powys) (Temporary 
Prohibition of Vehicles) Order 2018 
Made 
1 May 2018 
Coming into force 
8 May 2018 
The Welsh Ministers, as traffic authority for the 
relevant length of the A483 Swansea to Manchester 
Trunk Road, are satisfied that traffic on a specified 
length of the trunk road should be prohibited because 
of works proposed on or near the road. 
The Welsh Ministers, therefore, in exercise of the 
powers conferred upon them by section 14(1), (4) and 
(7) of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(1), make 
this Order. 
Title, Commencement and Interpretation 
1. The title of this Order is the A483 Trunk Road 
(West Street, Builth Wells, Powys) (Temporary 
Prohibition of Vehicles) Order 2018 and it comes into 
force on 8 May 2018. 
2.—(1) In this Order: 
“the trunk road” (“y gefnffordd”) means the A483 
Swansea to Manchester Trunk Road; 
“works period” (“cyfnod y gwaith”) means the 
period commencing at 19:30 hours on 8 May 2018 
and ending when the temporary traffic signs are 
permanently removed.

Prohibition 
3. No person may, during the works period, drive or 
cause or permit any vehicle to proceed westbound on 
the length of the trunk road known as West Street, 
Builth Wells, Powys that extends from a point 10 
metres west of the centre-point of its junction with 
Market Street to a point 5 metres east of the centre-
point of its junction with Smithfield Road. 
Suspension 
4. The provisions of the Trunk Road (A483) (Builth 
Wells, Powys) (One Way Traffic) Order 1983(2) are 
suspended in respect of the lengths of the trunk road 
known as High Street, Church Street and West Street 
during such times as the prohibition specified in article 
3 of this Order is in operation. 
Application 
5. The prohibition in article 3 applies only during 
such times and to such extent as indicated by traffic 
signs. 
Duration of this Order 
6. The maximum duration of this Order is 18 
months.
