ROAD TRAFFIC, WALES 
The A479 Trunk Road (Glanusk 
Park to Bronllys, Powys) 
(Temporary Prohibition of Vehicles) 
Order 2018 
Made 
11 September 2018 
Coming into force 
17 September 2018 
The Welsh Ministers, as traffic authority for the A479 
Trunk Road, are satisfied that traffic on a specified 
length of the trunk road should be prohibited because 
of works proposed on or near the road. 
The Welsh Ministers, therefore, in exercise of the 
powers conferred upon them by section 14(1) and (4) 
of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(1), make this 
Order. 
Title, Commencement and Interpretation  
1. The title of this Order is the A479 Trunk Road 
(Glanusk Park to Bronllys, Powys) (Temporary 
Prohibition of Vehicles) Order 2018 and it comes into 
force on 17 September 2018. 
2. In this Order: 
“the trunk road” (“y gefnffordd”) means the A479 
Glanusk Park to Llyswen Trunk Road; 
“works period” (“cyfnod y gwaith”) means 
intermittent periods commencing at 00:01 hours on 
17 September 2018 and ending when the 
temporary traffic signs for each period of works 
are permanently removed. 
                                                                               
(1) 
1984 c.27; section 14 was substituted by the Road Traffic 
(Temporary Restrictions) Act 1991 (c.26), section 1(1) and 
Schedule 1. By virtue of S.I. 1999/672, and section 162 of,

Wales Act 2006 (c.32), these powers are now exercisable by

2
Prohibition 
3. No person may, during the works period, drive or 
cause or permit any vehicle to proceed on the length of 
the trunk road that extends from the centre-point of its 
junction with the A40 at Glanusk Park to the 
roundabout junction with the A438 at Bronllys. 
Application 
4. The prohibition in article 3 applies only during 
such times and to such extent as indicated by traffic 
signs. 
Duration of this Order 
5. The maximum duration of this Order is 18 
months.
