The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Articles 10(4) and 13(1), (13) and (16) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern 
Ireland) Order 1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
It appears to the Department to be necessary to provide a suitable parking place for vehicles on the 
length of the road specified in the Schedule. 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 4 to the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 1997. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
The Department in compliance with paragraph 3(4) of that Schedule, has taken such other steps as 
appear to the Department reasonably practicable for the purpose of bringing specifically to the 
knowledge of the occupiers of land adjacent to the parking place the particulars specified in 
paragraph 3(2) of that Schedule. 
No written objection or other representation has been received. 
Citation, commencement and interpretation 
1.—(1) This Order may be cited as The Parking Places on Roads (Coaches) (Bangor) Order 
(Northern Ireland) 2019 and shall come into operation on 31st July 2019. 
(2) In this Order— 
“coach” means a motor vehicle with a maximum speed exceeding 60 miles per hour which is 
constructed or adapted to carry more than 16 seated persons in addition to the driver, and 
which is not used for the provision of a scheduled local service. 
“medical practitioner” means a registered person within the meaning of the Medical Act

Authorisation of a parking place 
2. The use as a parking place of the length of road specified in the Schedule is authorised. 
Use of a parking place 
3. Subject to Article 6, a vehicle other than a coach shall not wait in the parking place during the 
specified hours. 
Maximum period of waiting and further use of a parking place 
4.—(1) Save as provided in Article 6, a person shall not leave or cause— 
(a) a coach to be left in the parking place specified in the Schedule for longer than 20 
minutes. 
(b) that coach to be left again in that parking place until the expiration of 1 hour from the time 
that coach is removed from that parking place. 
Position within a parking place 
5. Save as provided in Article 6, a person shall not leave or cause a vehicle to be left in the 
parking place unless it is wholly within a parking space where such has been marked out. 
Exceptions 
6. Nothing in Articles 3, 4 or 5 shall render it unlawful to leave or cause a vehicle to be left in 
the parking place during the specified hours where the person in control of it is acting upon the 
direction or with the permission of a constable in uniform or is prevented from proceeding by 
circumstances beyond that person’s control or to avoid an accident or for so long as may be 
reasonably necessary to enable— 
(a) a person to board or alight from a vehicle provided that such boarding or alighting does 
not cause that vehicle to be left in that parking place for more than 2 minutes; 
(b) the vehicle to be used in an emergency by a medical practitioner, or for fire and rescue, 
ambulance, police or customs purposes or for the purposes of the security forces; 
(c) the vehicle to be used by or on behalf of a universal service provider for the purposes of 
the delivery to, or collection from, premises situated adjacent to the parking place of 
postal packets; 
(d) the vehicle, if it cannot reasonably be used for such purpose outside that parking place, to 
be used— 
(i) in connection with any building, repair or demolition operations; 
(ii) in connection with the removal of any obstruction to traffic;

Prohibition of the use of a suspended parking place 
8. A person not using a vehicle in connection with any of the undertakings specified in Article 7 
shall not leave or cause that vehicle to be left in the parking place where the use of that parking 
place has been suspended by the Department in accordance with that Article. 
Removal of a vehicle in an emergency 
9. A person authorised by the Department may remove a vehicle from the parking place in an 
emergency. 
Method of removal of a vehicle 
10. Any person removing a vehicle by virtue of Article 9 may do so by towing, or driving the 
vehicle or in such other manner as is reasonably necessary and may take such measures as are 
reasonably necessary to enable that person to remove it.

SCHEDULE 
Article 2 
PARKING PLACE FOR COACHES IN BANGOR (All days, between 
the hours of 7.00 a.m. and 7.00 p.m. with waiting limited to 20 minutes 
and no return within 1 hour) 
 
Bridge Street (north-west side of the north-west carriageway) – from a point 26 metres north-east 
of its junction with Queens Parade for a distance of 38 metres in a north-easterly direction. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order authorises the use as a parking place of the length of road specified in the Schedule 
(Article 2), restricts the use of that parking place to coaches during the specified hours (Article 3) 
and prescribes the conditions under which the parking place may be used (Articles 4, 5 and 8). 
Vehicles are excepted from the conditions in certain circumstances (Article 6). The Order also 
makes provision for the suspension of the use of the parking place (Article 7) and for the removal 
of a vehicle from the parking place in an emergency (Articles 9 and 10) and for its safe custody 
(Article 11). 
A penalty charge (£90) is payable with respect to a vehicle involved in a contravention of the 
Order in accordance with the Traffic Management (Northern Ireland) Order 2005 and The Penalty 
Charges (Prescribed Amounts) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2006. 
Traffic signs indicating the effect of the Order will in due course be placed on the road.
