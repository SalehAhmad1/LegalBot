The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Article 4(1), (2) and (3) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 
1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 1 to that Order. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
Two written objections were received and duly considered. Two representations in support were 
received. 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as The One-Way Traffic (Downpatrick) Order (Northern Ireland) 
2019 and shall come into operation on 12th August 2019. 
Prohibition 
2. A person shall not, except upon the direction or with the permission of a constable in uniform, 
cause or permit any vehicle to proceed on the lengths of road specified in Column 1 of Schedule 1 
in a direction other than that specified in relation to those roads in Column 2. 
Revocations 
3. The Orders specified in Schedule 2 are revoked.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order introduces a one-way traffic system on the length of Stream Street, Downpatrick 
specified in Schedule 1. 
The Order also revokes and re-enacts the provisions of the Orders specified in Schedule 2. 
Any person who acts in contravention of this Order shall be guilty of an offence under the Road 
Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 1997 (“the Regulation Order”) and shall be liable on 
summary conviction to a fine not exceeding level 3 on the standard scale (£1,000) in accordance 
with The Road Traffic Offenders (Northern Ireland) Order 1996 as amended by the Regulation 
Order. 
Traffic signs indicating the effect of this Order will in due course be placed on the road.
