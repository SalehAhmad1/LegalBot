The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Article 4(1) and (2) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 1997(b) 
and now vested in it(c). 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 1 to that Order. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
No objection or other representation has been received. 
Citation, commencement and interpretation 
1.—(1) This Order may be cited as the Traffic Weight Restriction (Augher) Order (Northern 
Ireland) 2024 and shall come into operation on 22nd March 2024. 
(2) In this Order— 
“goods” includes goods or burden of any description;  
“maximum gross weight” has the meaning assigned to that term by regulation 2 of the Traffic 
Signs Regulations (Northern Ireland) 1997(d); 
“medical practitioner” means a registered person within the meaning of the Medical Act 
1983(e); and 
“postal packet” has the same meaning as in section 65(1) Postal Services Act 2011(f).

Prohibition 
2. Subject to Article 3, the use by vehicles exceeding 18 tonnes maximum gross weight of a 
length of the Lisnawery Road, Augher Unclassified 0337, Stretch 2, from its junction with Favour 
Royal Road to its junction with Lisnawery Road, Augher, Unclassified 0337, Stretch 5 is 
prohibited. 
Exceptions 
3. The prohibition in Article 2 shall not apply to— 
(a) vehicles being used— 
(i) by or on behalf of the owner or occupier of land abutting on or convenient to the 
length of road for the purpose of gaining access to such land; or 
(ii) in connection with the delivery to or collection from such land of any goods or 
livestock or postal packets; or 
(iii) in an emergency by a medical practitioner, or for fire and rescue, ambulance, police 
or customs purposes or for the purposes of the security forces; or 
(iv) on or adjacent length of road in pursuance of statutory powers and duties by or on 
behalf of a district council or government department; or 
(v) by or on behalf of a sewerage undertaker or a water undertaker in connection with 
the discharge of that undertaker’s functions; or 
(vi) in connection with the laying, erection, alteration or repair of any electronic 
communication apparatus as defined in Schedule 2 to the Telecommunications Act 
1984(a); or 
(vii) on or adjacent to the length of road by or on behalf of the holder of a licence granted 
under Article 10 of the Electricity (Northern Ireland) Order 1992(b) or of a licence 
granted under Article 8 of the Gas (Northern Ireland) Order 1996(c) in connection 
with the discharge of the holder’s functions; or 
(viii) agricultural vehicles for agricultural purposes;  
(b) vehicles providing a passenger transport service.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order prohibits vehicles exceeding 18 tonnes maximum gross weight from using a length of 
the Lisnawery Road, Augher. 
Vehicles requiring access are excepted from the provisions of the Order. 
Any person who acts in contravention of the Order shall be guilty of an offence under the Road 
Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 1997 (“the Regulation Order”) and shall be liable on 
summary conviction to a fine not exceeding level 3 on the standard scale (£1,000), in accordance 
with the Road Traffic Offenders (Northern Ireland) Order 1996 as amended by the Regulation 
Order. 
Traffic signs indicating the effect of the Order will be placed on the road in due course.
