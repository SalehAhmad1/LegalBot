The Department for Infrastructure (a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Article 68(1) and (5) of the Roads (Northern Ireland) Order 1993(b) and now vested 
in it(c). 
The Department in accordance with Article 68(4) of that Order proposes to abandon the area of 
road described in the Schedule as it is not necessary for road traffic. 
Notice has been published, served and displayed in compliance with paragraphs 1, 2 and 3 of 
Schedule 8 to that Order. 
No objection has been received. 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as the Carrickmannon Road, Crossgar (Abandonment) Order 
(Northern Ireland) 2024 and shall come into operation on 24th April 2024. 
Application 
2. The area of road described in the Schedule is abandoned. 
3.—(1) All existing cables, wires, pipes or other apparatus owned by BT Group plc and 
Northern Ireland Water Limited placed along, across, over or under the abandoned area of road 
shall be retained. 
(2) All existing rights as to the use or maintenance of such cables, wires, mains pipes or other 
apparatus shall be preserved. 
4.—(1) All existing cables, wires, mains, pipes or other apparatus owned by the Department for 
the provision of street lighting, placed along, across, over or under the abandoned area of road 
shall be relocated.

SCHEDULE 
Article 2 
AREA OF ROAD TO BE ABANDONED 
An area of 231 square metres of superseded road at Carrickmannon Road, Crossgar commencing 
at a point 26 metres east of its junction with Manse Road and continuing for a distance of 35 
metres in a northerly direction, more particularly delineated and shown cross-hatched and 
coloured red on map number IN1/23/633643. 
A copy of the map has been deposited at the Department’s Headquarters, Room 309, Clarence 
Court, 10-18 Adelaide Street, Belfast and at DfI Roads Southern Division, Newry Mourne & 
Down East Section Office, 129 Newcastle Road, Seaforde, Downpatrick. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
EXPLANATORY NOTE
