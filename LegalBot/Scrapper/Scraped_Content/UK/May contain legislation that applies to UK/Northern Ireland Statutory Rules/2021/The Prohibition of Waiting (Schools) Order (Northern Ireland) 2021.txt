The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Article 4(1), (2) and (3) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 
1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 1 to that Order. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
No written objection or other representation has been received. 
Citation, commencement and interpretation 
1.—(1) This Order may be cited as The Prohibition of Waiting (Schools) Order (Northern 
Ireland) 2021 and shall come into operation on 15th February 2021. 
(2) In this Order— 
“specified road” means the carriageway of a length of road specified in column 1 of Schedule 
1 or column 1 of Schedule 2. 
Prohibition of waiting 
2. Save as provided in Article 3, no person shall, except upon the direction or with the 
permission of a constable in uniform, cause or permit a vehicle to wait wholly or partly in a 
specified road— 
(a) during the hours and on the days specified in columns 2 and 3 respectively of Schedule 1; 
or 
(b) during the hours and on the days and dates specified in columns 2, 3 and 4 respectively of 
Schedule 2. 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) Formerly the Department for Regional Development; see section 1(6) and (11) of, and Schedule 1 to, the Departments Act 
(Northern Ireland) 2016 (2016 c. 5 (N.I.)) 
(b) S.I. 1997/276 (N.I. 2) 
(c) S.R. 1999 No. 481 Article 6(d) and Schedule 4 Part IV 
 
2
Exceptions 
3. The prohibition in Article 2 shall not render it unlawful for any person to cause or permit any 
vehicle to stop in a specified road so far as such stopping is reasonably necessary for the 
avoidance of an accident or to enable the vehicle to be used— 
(a) for police, military, fire and rescue or ambulance purposes; 
(b) in the services of the Department or a district council in pursuance of statutory powers or 
duties; 
(c) in connection with the supply of electricity or gas or any telegraphic line; or 
(d) in connection with the removal of any obstruction to traffic. 
Revocations 
4. The provisions specified in column 1 of Schedule 3 are revoked to the extent specified in 
column 3.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This order prohibits waiting by vehicles, with certain exceptions, on lengths of Church Road, 
Ballymena; Glenburn Park, Bangor; Main Road, Glynn and Moyard Gardens, Greenisland, during 
the times and on the days and during the period specified in item nos. 6, 13, 29 and 30 of Schedule 
2. 
The Order also revokes prohibitions on lengths of Blackcave Road, Larne at the former Corran 
Integrated Primary School and School Road, Belfast at the former Newtownbreda Primary School 
as they are no longer required and revokes and re-enacts the other prohibitions contained in the 
provisions of the Orders specified in Schedule 3. 
A penalty charge (£90) is payable with respect to a vehicle involved in a contravention of this 
Order in accordance with The Traffic Management (Northern Ireland) Order 2005 and The 
Penalty Charges (Prescribed Amounts) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2006. 
Traffic signs indicating the effect of this Order will in due course be placed on the roads.
