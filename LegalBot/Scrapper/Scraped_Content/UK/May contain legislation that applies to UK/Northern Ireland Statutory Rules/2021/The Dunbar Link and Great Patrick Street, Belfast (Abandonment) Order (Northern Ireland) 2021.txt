Coming into operation -

The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Articles 68(1) and (5) of the Roads (Northern Ireland) Order 1993(b) and now vested 
in it(c). 
The Department in accordance with Article 68(4) of that Order proposes to abandon the areas of 
road described in the Schedule as they are not necessary. 
Notice has been published, served and displayed in compliance with paragraphs 1, 2 and 3 of 
Schedule 8 to that Order. 
No objection has been received. 
Citation and commencement 
1. This Order may be cited as The Dunbar Link and Great Patrick Street, Belfast (Abandonment) 
Order (Northern Ireland) 2021 and shall come into operation on 23rd June 2021. 
Application 
2. The areas of road described in the Schedule is abandoned. 
3.—(1) All existing cables, wires, mains, pipes or other apparatus placed along, across, over or 
under the abandoned area of road shall be retained. 
(2) All existing rights as to the use or maintenance of such cables, wires, mains, pipes or other 
apparatus shall be preserved.

SCHEDULE 
Article 2 
AREAS OF ROAD TO BE ABANDONED 
Two areas of former footpath totalling 253.24 square metres at Dunbar Link, Belfast comprising 
of— 
(a) 147.843 square metres of former footpath at the junction of Dunbar Link and Great 
Patrick Street (hatched red on map number IN1/21/86944); and 
(b) 105.397 square metres of former footpath at the junction of Dunbar Link and Exchange 
Street (cross hatched green on map number IN1/21/86944). 
A copy of the map has been deposited at the Department’s Headquarters, Room 301, Clarence 
Court, 10-18 Adelaide Street, Belfast and at DfI Roads and Rivers Eastern Division, Annexe 7 
Block 2, Castle Buildings, Stormont Estate, Upper Newtownards Road, Belfast. 
 
 
 
EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order abandons the areas of road described in the Schedule.
