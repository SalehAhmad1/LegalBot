The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Articles 10(4) and 13(1), (13) and (16) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern 
Ireland) Order 1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
It appears to the Department to be necessary to provide a suitable parking place for vehicles on the 
length of the road specified in Schedules 1 and 2 to this Order. 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 4 to the Road Traffic Regulation Order (Northern Ireland) 1997. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
The Department in compliance with paragraph 3(4) of that Schedule has taken such other steps as 
appear to the Department reasonably practicable for the purpose of bring specifically to the 
knowledge of occupiers of land adjacent to the parking place the particulars specified in paragraph 
3(2) of that Schedule. 
No written objection or other representation has been received.

Authorisation of a parking place 
2. The use as a parking place of the length of road specified in Schedules 1 and 2 is authorised. 
Position within a parking place 
3. Subject to Article 10, a person shall not leave or cause a vehicle to be left in the parking place 
unless it is wholly within a parking space where such has been marked out. 
Suspension of the use of a parking place 
4. The Department may suspend the use of the parking place or any part of it for so long as may 
be necessary for the purpose of enabling— 
(a) A vehicle, if it cannot conveniently be used for such purpose outside the parking place, to 
be used in connection with any of the following purposes— 
(i) building, repair or demolition operations, including works to property adjacent to the 
parking place; 
(ii) the removal of any obstruction to traffic; 
(iii) the cleansing, maintenance, improvement or reconstruction of the road; or 
(iv) the laying, erection, alteration or repair in, or in land adjacent to the road, of any 
sewer, main, pipe or apparatus for the supply of gas, water or electricity or of 
electronic communications apparatus as defined in Schedule 2 to the 
Telecommunications Act 1984(c); 
(b) the implementation of any traffic management scheme; or 
(c) street works as defined by Article 3(3) of the Street Works (Northern Ireland) Order 
1995(d) to be executed. 
Prohibition of the use of a suspended parking place 
5. A person not using a vehicle in connection with any of the undertakings specified in Article 4 
shall not leave or cause that vehicle to be left in the parking place where the use of that parking 
place has been suspended by the Department in accordance with that Article. 
Removal of a vehicle in an emergency 
6. A person authorised by the Department may remove a vehicle from the parking place in an 
emergency.

Method of removal of a vehicle 
7. Any person removing a vehicle by virtue of Article 6 may do so by towing or driving the 
vehicle or, in such a manner as is reasonably necessary and may take such measures as are 
reasonably necessary to enable that person to remove it. 
Custody of a vehicle 
8. When a person authorised by the Department removes or makes arrangements for the removal 
of a vehicle from the parking place by virtue of Article 6 that person shall make such 
arrangements as may be necessary for the safe custody of the vehicle. 
Restricted use (disabled bay) of the parking place (Schedule 1) 
9. Subject to Article 11, a vehicle other than a disabled person’s vehicle shall not wait in the 
parking place specified in Schedule 1 between the hours of 11.00 a.m. and 6.00 p.m. on any day  
where— 
(a) a person shall not leave or cause a vehicle to be left in the parking place for longer than 3 
hours; and 
(b) a person shall not leave or cause that vehicle to be left again in that parking place until the 
expiration of 1 hour from the time that vehicle is removed from that parking place. 
Restricted use (loading bay) of the parking place (Schedule 2) 
10. Subject to Article 11, between the hours of 6.00 p.m. and 11.00 a.m. on any day, the driver 
of a vehicle shall not use the parking place specified in Schedule 2 other than for the purpose of 
the conveyance of goods to or from premises situated adjacent to that parking place where— 
(a) the goods by reason of their size or weight could not reasonably be conveyed to or from 
such premises otherwise than by means of a vehicle; and 
(b) the use is no longer than is necessary to convey those goods to or from those premises.  
Exceptions to use as a parking place 
11. Nothing in Articles 3, 9 or 10 shall render it unlawful to leave or cause a vehicle to be left in 
the parking place where the person in control of it is prevented from proceeding by circumstances 
beyond that person’s control or to avoid an accident or for so long as may be reasonably necessary 
to enable— 
(a) the vehicle being used in an emergency by a medical practitioner, or for fire and rescue, 
ambulance, police or customs purposes or for the purposes of the security forces; 
(b) the vehicle to be used by or on behalf of a universal service provider for the purposes of 
the delivery to or collection from premises situated adjacent to the parking place of postal 
packets; 
(c) the vehicle, if it cannot be conveniently used for such purposes outside that parking place, 
to be used— 
(i) in connection with any building, repair or demolition operations; 
(ii) in connection with the removal of any obstruction to traffic; 
(iii) by or on behalf of a district council or a government department in pursuance of 
statutory powers or duties; 
(iv) by or on behalf of a sewerage undertaker or a water undertaker in connection with 
the discharge of that undertaker’s functions;

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order authorises the use as a parking place of the length of road specified in the Schedules 
(Article 2) and prescribes the conditions under which it may be used (Articles 9 and 10.) Vehicles 
are excepted from the conditions in certain circumstances (Article 11). 
Between the hours of 11.00 a.m. and 6.00 p.m. the parking place may be used as a disabled bay 
and between the hours of 6.00 p.m. and 11.00 a.m. the parking place may be used as a loading 
bay. 
The Order will also revoke certain existing parking places on Castle Place, Belfast (Article 12). 
A penalty charge (£90) is payable with respect to a vehicle involved in a contravention of the 
Order in accordance with the Traffic Management (Northern Ireland) Order 2005 and the Penalty 
Charges (Prescribed Amounts) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2006. 
Traffic signs indicating the effect of this Order will in due course be placed on the road.
