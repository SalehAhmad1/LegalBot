The Department for Infrastructure(a) makes the following Order in exercise of the powers 
conferred by Article 4(1), (2) and (3) of the Road Traffic Regulation (Northern Ireland) Order 
1997(b) and now vested in it(c). 
The Department has consulted such persons as it considered appropriate in compliance with 
paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 1 to that Order. 
The Department has published a notice in compliance with paragraphs 1 and 3 of that Schedule. 
No written objection or other representation has been received. 
Citation, commencement and interpretation 
1.—(1) This Order may be cited as the Prohibition of Traffic (Brunswick Street, Belfast) Order 
(Northern Ireland) 2022 and shall come into operation on 28th December 2022. 
(2) In this Order— 
“cycle” has the same meaning as in the Order of 1995(d); 
“medical practitioner” means a registered person within the meaning of the Medical Act 
1983(e); 
“postal packet” has the same meaning as in section 65(1) of the Postal Services Act 2011(f); 
and 
“universal service provider” has the same meaning as in section 65(1) of the Postal Services 
Act 2011.

Prohibition 
2. Subject to Article 3 the use by vehicles of Brunswick Street, Belfast from its junction with 
James Street South to its junction with Franklin Street is prohibited. 
Exceptions 
3. The prohibition in Article 2 shall not apply to— 
(a) a cycle; 
(b) a vehicle the driver or rider of which is acting upon the direction, or with the permission 
of, a constable in uniform; 
(c) a vehicle to be used in an emergency by a medical practitioner, or for fire and rescue, 
ambulance, police or customs purposes pr for the purposes of the security forces; 
(d) a vehicle to be used by or on behalf of a universal service provider for the purposes of the 
delivery to, or collection from, premises situated adjacent to the length of road of postal 
packets; 
(e) a vehicle, if it cannot conveniently be used for such purposes outside that length of the 
road, to be used— 
(i) in connection with any building, repair or demolition operations; 
(ii) in connection with the removal of any obstruction to traffic; 
(iii) by or on behalf of a district council or a government department in pursuance of 
statutory powers or duties; 
(iv) by or on behalf of a sewerage undertaker or a water undertaker in connection with 
the discharge of that undertaker’s functions; 
(v) in connection with the discharge of the functions of the holder of a licence granted 
under Article 10 of the Electricity (Northern Ireland) Order 1992(a) or of a licence 
granted under Article 8 of the Gas (Northern Ireland) Order 1996(b); or 
(vi) in connection with the laying, erection, alteration or repair of any electronic 
telecommunications apparatus as defined in Schedule 2 to the Telecommunications 
Act 1984(c). 
Amendment to the on-street parking order 
4. The On-Street Parking Order (Northern Ireland)(d) is amended by the substitution for item 
no. 19 relating to Brunswick Street in Chapter 1, Part 1 of Schedule 1 of the same description 
together with a new map.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Order) 
This Order prohibits vehicles from using Brunswick Street, Belfast from its junction with James 
Street South to its junction with Franklin Street. Certain vehicles are excepted from the prohibition 
in certain circumstances. 
The Order will also amend The On-Street Parking Order (Northern Ireland) 2000 by the removal 
of certain pay and display parking bays on Brunswick Street. 
A penalty charge (£90) is payable with respect to a vehicle involved in a contravention of the 
Order in accordance with the Traffic Management (Northern Ireland) Order 2005 and the Penalty 
Charges (Prescribed Amounts) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2006. 
Traffic signs indicating the effect of this Order will in due course be placed on the road.
