The Scottish Ministers make the following Scheme in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
7, 8(2) and 10(1) of the Roads (Scotland) Act 1984(a) and all other powers enabling them to do 
so. 
In accordance with section 7(7) of that Act, they have given due consideration to the requirements 
of local and national planning, and to the requirements of agriculture and industry and are satisfied 
as to expediency. 
In accordance with sections 20C and 55A of that Act(b) they have determined that the project falls 
within Annex I to the Directive 2011/92/EU of the European Parliament and the Council(c) on the 
assessment of certain public and private projects on the environment, and have published notice of 
that determination on 27 August 2020. 
They prepared an Environmental Impact Assessment Report and published notice of it on 27th 
August 2020. 
This Order is made in compliance with the provisions of Parts II and III of Schedule 1(d) to the 
Roads (Scotland) Act 1984.

2
Citation and commencement 
1. This Scheme may be cited as the M9/A9 Trunk Road (Winchburgh Junction 1B at the B8020) 
Special Road Scheme 2021 and comes into force on 25 November 2021. 
Special Road 
2. The definitions in the schedule to this Scheme have effect. 
3. The Scottish Ministers, as special road authority are, by means of this Scheme, authorised to 
provide a special road along the route described in the schedule to this Scheme and such road shall 
become trunk road on the date this Scheme comes into force. 
Classes of Traffic 
4. Classes I and II of the classes of traffic set out in schedule 3 to the Roads (Scotland) Act 1984 
are prescribed for the purpose of the use of the special road, the route of which is described in the 
schedule.

SCHEDULE 
Articles 2, 3 and 4 
INTERPRETATION 
In this schedule— 
“proposed B8020 link road” means the proposed new local road linking the B8020 south of 
the M9 to the B8020 north of the M9, including two new roundabouts; 
“M9” means the M9/A9 Edinburgh – Stirling – Thurso Trunk Road at Winchburgh; 
“Reference Point A” means the point of the intersection of the centreline of the proposed 
B8020 link road and the M9 overbridge as shown on plans PS1 and PS2 in the plan folio; 
“the plan folio” means the plan folio numbered UM/NSE/M9/J1B and entitled “The M9/A9 
Trunk Road (Winchburgh Junction 1B at B8020) Special Road Scheme 2021” signed with 
reference to this Scheme and deposited at the offices of Transport Scotland, Buchanan House, 
58 Port Dundas Road, Glasgow, G4 0HF. 
 
THE ROUTES OF THE SPECIAL ROADS 
1. From a point 725 metres or thereby north west of Reference Point A in a generally south 
easterly direction for a distance of 648 metres or thereby to a point 100 metres or thereby north 
west of Reference Point A on the proposed roundabout north of the M9, as shown by a heavy 
black line marked “1” on plan PS1 in the plan folio. 
2. From a point 620 metres or thereby north west of Reference Point A in a generally south 
easterly direction for a distance of 678 metres or thereby to a point 96 metres or thereby south 
west of Reference Point A on the proposed roundabout south of the M9, as shown by a heavy 
black line marked “2” on plan PS1 in the plan folio. 
3. From a point 568 metres or thereby south east of Reference Point A in a generally north 
westerly direction for a distance of 609 metres or thereby to a point 75 metres or thereby north of 
Reference Point A on the proposed roundabout north of the M9, as shown by a heavy black line 
marked “3” on plan PS2 in the plan folio. 
4. From a point 835 metres or thereby south east of Reference Point A in a generally north 
westerly direction for a distance of 742 metres or thereby to a point 120 metres or thereby south 
east of Reference Point A on the proposed roundabout south of the M9, as shown by a heavy black 
line marked “4” on plan PS2 in the plan folio.
