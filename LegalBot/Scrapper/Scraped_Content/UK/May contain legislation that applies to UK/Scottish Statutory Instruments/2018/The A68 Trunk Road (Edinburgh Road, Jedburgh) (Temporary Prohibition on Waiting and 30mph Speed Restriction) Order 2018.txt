The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
2(1) and (2), 4(1) and 14(1)(b) and (4) of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(a) and all other 
powers enabling them to do so. 
In accordance with section 14(1)(b) of that Act, they are satisfied that the traffic on the lengths of 
road specified in the schedule should be restricted or prohibited because of the likelihood of 
danger to the public, which is not attributable to works. 
In accordance with section 14(3) of that Act, they have had regard to the existence of alternative 
routes suitable for the traffic which will be affected by this Order. 
Citation, commencement and cessation 
1. This Order may be cited as the A68 Trunk Road (Edinburgh Road, Jedburgh) (Temporary 
Prohibition on Waiting and 30mph Speed Restriction) Order 2018, comes into force on 19th May 
2018 and ceases to have effect on 20th May 2018. 
Prohibition on waiting 
2. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle to wait on the length of road, or a part or 
parts of the length of road, specified in Part 1 of the schedule when a prohibition is identified as 
applying to that length of road, or a part or parts of that length of road, by means of a traffic sign 
of the type described in item 19 (diagram 636 – temporary prohibition on waiting except for 
loading and unloading) of the sign table in Part 6 of schedule 13 of the Traffic Signs Regulations 
and General Directions 2016(b).

Speed restriction 
3. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on the length of road, or part or parts of 
the length of road, specified in Part 2 of the schedule at a speed exceeding 30 miles per hour when 
a restriction is identified as applying to that length of road, or part or parts of that length of road, 
by means of a traffic sign of the type described in item 1 (diagram 670 – maximum speed limit in 
mph) of the sign table in Part 2 of schedule 10 of the Traffic Signs Regulations and General 
Directions 2016(a). 
Exemptions 
4. No prohibition imposed by this Order applies to a vehicle being used in an emergency for fire 
brigade, ambulance, police or coastguard purposes. 
5. No speed restriction imposed by this Order applies to— 
(a) vehicles falling within regulation 3(4) of the Road Traffic Exemptions (Special Forces) 
(Variation and Amendment) Regulations 2011(b) when used in accordance with 
regulation 3(5) of those Regulations; or 
(b) vehicles being used in an emergency for coastguard purposes.

PART 2
