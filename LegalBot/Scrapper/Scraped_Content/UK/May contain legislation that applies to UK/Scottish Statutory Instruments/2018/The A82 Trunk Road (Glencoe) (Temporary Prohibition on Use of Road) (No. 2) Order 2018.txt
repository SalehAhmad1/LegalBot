The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
2(1) and (2), 4(1) and 14(1)(b) and (4) of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(a) and all other 
powers enabling them to do so. 
In accordance with section 14(1)(b) of that Act, they are satisfied that traffic on the length of road 
specified in the schedule should be restricted or prohibited because of the likelihood of danger to 
the public, or of serious damage to the road, which is not attributable to works. 
In accordance with section 14(3) of that Act, they have had regard to the existence of alternative 
routes suitable for the traffic which will be affected by this Order. 
Citation, commencement and cessation 
1. This Order may be cited as the A82 Trunk Road (Glencoe) (Temporary Prohibition on Use of 
Road) (No. 2) Order 2018, comes into force on 16th September 2018 and ceases to have effect on 
17th September 2018. 
Prohibition on use of road 
2. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on the length of road, or a part or parts 
of the length of road, specified in the schedule of this Order when a prohibition is identified as 
applying to that length of road, or a part or parts of that length of road, by means of a traffic sign 
of the type described in item 8 (diagram 7105 – position of barrier to mark length of road closed or 
to guide traffic past an obstruction) of the sign table in Part 6 of schedule 13 of the Traffic Signs 
Regulations and General Directions 2016(b).

Exemption 
3. No prohibition imposed by this Order applies to a vehicle being used in an emergency for fire 
brigade, ambulance, police or coastguard purposes.

SCHEDULE 
Article 2 
SPECIFIED LENGTH OF ROAD 
1. The A82 Dalnottar – Inverness Trunk Road from a point about 2.5 kilometres or thereby west of 
its junction with the ZC1094 Glen Etive Road in a generally westwards direction to a point about 
3 kilometres or thereby west of the said junction, a distance of 500 metres or thereby. 
2. The A82 Dalnottar – Inverness Trunk Road from a point about 500 metres or thereby east of its 
junction with the Old Glencoe and Clachaig Inn Road in a generally westwards direction to a point 
about 3.5 kilometres or thereby west of the said junction, a distance of 4 kilometres or thereby.
