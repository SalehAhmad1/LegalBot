The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
2(1) and (2), 4(1) and 14(1)(b) and (4), of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(a), and all other 
powers enabling them to do so. 
In accordance with section 14(1)(b) of that Act, they are satisfied that the traffic on the lengths of 
road specified in the schedule should be restricted or prohibited because of the likelihood of 
danger to the public, or of serious damage to the road, which is not attributable to works. 
In accordance with section 14(3) of that Act, they have had regard to the existence of alternative 
routes suitable for traffic which will be affected by this Order. 
Citation, commencement and cessation 
1. This Order may be cited as the M9/A9 Trunk Road (Solheim Cup) (Temporary Prohibition on 
Use of Road, of Waiting and Specified Turns and Temporary 50mph and 30mph Speed 
Restrictions) Order 2019, comes into force on 26 August 2019 and ceases to have effect on 22 
September 2019. 
Prohibition on use of road 
2. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on a length of road, or part or parts of a 
length of road, specified in Part 1 of the schedule when a prohibition is identified as applying to 
that length of road, or a part or parts of that length of road, by means of a traffic sign of the type 
described in item 8 (diagram 7105 – position of barrier to mark length of road closed or to guide 
traffic past an obstruction) of the sign table in Part 6 of schedule 13 of the Traffic Signs 
Regulations and General Directions 2016(b).

Prohibition of waiting 
3. No person shall cause or permit any vehicle to wait at any time on a length of road, or a part 
or parts of a length of road, specified in Part 2 of the schedule, when such prohibition is identified 
as applying to a length of road or any part or parts of a length of road by means of a traffic sign of 
the type described in item 19, (diagram 636 - temporary prohibition of waiting except for loading 
and unloading) of the sign table in Part 6 of schedule 13 of the Traffic Signs Regulations and 
General Directions 2016. 
Prohibition of specified turns 
4. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on the lengths of road specified in Part 2 
of the schedule so as to make any turn specified in Part 3 of the schedule when a prohibition is 
identified as applying to any part or parts of those lengths of road by means of a traffic sign of the 
type described in item 6 (diagram 614 – no u-turns for vehicular traffic) or item 7 (diagram 612 – 
no right turn for vehicular traffic) of the sign table in Part 2 of schedule 3 of the Traffic Signs 
Regulations and General Directions 2016. 
Speed restriction 
5. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on a length of road, or a part or parts of 
a length of road, specified in column 1 of the table in Part 4 of the schedule at a speed exceeding 
that specified in the corresponding entry in column 2 of the table when a restriction is identified as 
applying to that length of road, or a part or parts of that length of road, by means of a traffic sign 
of the type described in item 1 (diagram 670 – maximum speed limit in mph) of the sign table in 
Part 2 of schedule 10 of the Traffic Signs Regulations and General Directions 2016. 
Exemptions 
6. No prohibition imposed by this Order applies to vehicles being used in an emergency for fire 
brigade, ambulance, police or coastguard purposes. 
7. No speed restriction imposed by this Order applies to— 
(a) vehicles falling within regulation 3(4) of the Road Traffic Exemptions (Special Forces) 
(Variation and Amendment) Regulations 2011(a) when used in accordance with 
regulation 3(5) of those Regulations, or 
(b) vehicles being used in an emergency for coastguard purposes.
