The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by section 
62(1) of the Roads (Scotland) Act 1984(a) and all other powers enabling them to do so. 
In accordance with section 62(1) that Act, they are of the opinion that considerations of public 
safety or convenience require that traffic and foot passage should be temporarily prohibited on the 
length of road specified in the schedule while filming on the road takes place. 
Citation, commencement and cessation 
1. This Order may be cited as the A83 Trunk Road (Inveraray) (Temporary Prohibition of Use, 
Pedestrians and Waiting, Loading and Unloading) Order 2022, comes into force on 7 September 
2022 and ceases to have effect on 14 September 2022. 
Prohibition on use of road 
2. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on the length of road, or a part or parts 
of the length of road, specified in the schedule when a prohibition is identified as applying to that 
length of road, or a part or parts of that length of road, by means of a traffic sign of the type 
described in item 8 (diagram 7105 – position of barrier to mark length of road closed or to guide 
traffic past an obstruction) of the sign table in Part 6 of schedule 13 of the Traffic Signs 
Regulations and General Directions 2016(b). 
Prohibition of pedestrians  
3. No pedestrian may enter or proceed along the length of road, or part or parts of the length of 
road, specified in the schedule when a prohibition is identified as applying to that length of road, 
or part of parts of that length of road, by means of one or more of the traffic sign of the type 
described in item 22 (diagram 625.1 – Pedestrians prohibited) of the sign table in Part 2 of 
schedule 3 of the Traffic Signs Regulations and General Directions 2016(c).

Prohibition on waiting, loading and unloading 
4. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle to wait, load or unload on the length of 
road, or a part or parts of the length of road, specified in the schedule when a prohibition is 
identified as applying to that length of road, or a part or parts of that length of road, by means of 
one or both of the traffic signs of the type described in item 19 (diagram 636 - temporary 
prohibition of waiting except for loading and unloading) and item 20 (diagram 636.1 - temporary 
prohibition of loading and unloading) of the sign table in Part 6 of schedule 13 of the Traffic Signs 
Regulations and General Directions 2016(a). 
Exemptions 
5. No prohibition imposed by this Order applies to— 
(a) a vehicle being used in an emergency for fire brigade, ambulance, police or coastguard 
purposes,  
(b) a vehicle being used in connection with the filming on the road, or 
(c) a vehicle being used in connection with the placing of the required signage. 
6. No prohibition on pedestrians imposed by this Order applies to a person—  
(a) while engaged in connection with ambulance, police or coastguard purposes, or for or in 
connection with the exercise of any function of the Scottish Fire and Rescue Service, or 
(b) leaving a vehicle which is prevented from proceeding by circumstances beyond their 
control.

SCHEDULE 
Article 2, 3 and 4 
SPECIFIED LENGTH OF ROAD 
That length of the A83 Tarbet – Lochgilphead – Campbeltown Trunk Road at Inveraray from its 
junction with the entrance to the Fisher Row Car Park in a general north-easterly direction to its 
junction with Front Street East, a distance of 240 metres or thereby.
