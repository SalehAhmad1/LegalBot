The Scottish Ministers make the following Order in exercise of the powers conferred by sections 
2(1) and (2), 4(1) and 14(1)(b) and (4), of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(a), and all other 
powers enabling them to do so. 
In accordance with section 14(1)(b) of that Act, they are satisfied that the traffic on the lengths of 
road specified in the schedule should be restricted or prohibited because of the likelihood of 
danger to the public, or of serious damage to the road, which is not attributable to works. 
In accordance with section 14(3) of that Act, they have had regard to the existence of alternative 
routes suitable for traffic which will be affected by this Order. 
Citation, commencement and cessation 
1. This Order may be cited as the A92 and A96 Trunk Roads (Haudagain Improvement) 
(Temporary Prohibition of Waiting and Specified Turns and Temporary 20mph, 30mph and 
40mph Speed Restrictions) Order 2022, comes into force on 11 May 2022 and ceases to have 
effect on 31 March 2023. 
Prohibition of waiting 
2. No person shall cause or permit any vehicle to wait at any time on a length of road, or a part 
or parts of a length of road, specified in Part 1 of the schedule. 
Speed restriction 
3. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on a length of road, or a part or parts of 
a length of road, specified in column 1 of the table in Part 2 of the schedule at a speed exceeding

Prohibition of specified turns 
4. No person may use, or permit the use of, any vehicle on the lengths of road specified in Part 3 
of the schedule so as to make any turn specified in Part 4 of the schedule when a prohibition is 
identified as applying to any part or parts of those lengths of road by means of a traffic sign of the 
type described in item 7 (diagram 612 – no right turn for vehicular traffic) of the sign table in Part 
2 of schedule 3 of the Traffic Signs Regulations and General Directions 2016. 
Exemptions 
5. No prohibition imposed by this Order applies to vehicles being used in an emergency for fire 
brigade, ambulance, police or coastguard purposes. 
6. No speed restriction imposed by this Order applies to— 
(a) vehicles falling within regulation 3(4) of the Road Traffic Exemptions (Special Forces) 
(Variation and Amendment) Regulations 2011(b) when used in accordance with 
regulation 3(5) of those Regulations, or 
(b) vehicles being used in an emergency for coastguard purposes.

SCHEDULE 
Articles 2, 3 and 4 
Interpretation 
In this schedule— 
“A96 Auchmill Road” means the A96 Aberdeen – Inverness Trunk Road between the existing 
Auchmill Terrace, Aberdeen and the existing Haudagain Roundabout, Aberdeen, 
“A92 North Anderson Drive” means the M90/A90 Edinburgh – Fraserburgh Trunk Road 
between Middlefield Place, Aberdeen and the existing Haudagain Roundabout, Aberdeen, 
“A92/A96 Dual Carriageway Link Road” means the new road which links the existing 
M90/A90 Edinburgh – Fraserburgh Trunk Road, Aberdeen to the existing A96 Aberdeen – 
Inverness Trunk Road, Aberdeen in the vicinity of the existing Haudagain Roundabout, 
Aberdeen, 
“Haudagain Roundabout” means the roundabout at the junction of the existing M90/A90 
Edinburgh – Fraserburgh Trunk Road and the existing A96 Aberdeen – Inverness Trunk Road 
and the A96 Great Northern Road, Aberdeen, 
“Re-aligned Manor Avenue” means the new road which connects the existing Manor Avenue, 
Aberdeen to the A92/A96 Dual Carriageway Link Road, Aberdeen.
