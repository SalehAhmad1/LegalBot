ROAD TRAFFIC, WALES 
The A44 Trunk Road (West of 
Llangurig, Powys) (Temporary 
Prohibition of Vehicles) Order 2018 
Made 
16 October 2018 
Coming into force 
22 October 2018 
The Welsh Ministers, as traffic authority for the A44 
Trunk Road, are satisfied that traffic on a specified 
length of the trunk road should be prohibited because 
of works proposed on or near the road. 
The Welsh Ministers, therefore, in exercise of the 
powers conferred upon them by section 14(1) and (4) 
of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(1), make this 
Order. 
Title, Commencement and Interpretation  
1. The title of this Order is the A44 Trunk Road 
(West of Llangurig, Powys) (Temporary Prohibition of 
Vehicles) Order 2018 and it comes into force on 22 
October 2018. 
2. In this Order: 
“exempted vehicle” (“cerbyd esempt”) means any 
vehicle being used by the emergency services or in 
connection with the works giving rise to this 
Order; 
“the trunk road” (“y gefnffordd”) means the A44 
Newtown to Aberystwyth Trunk Road; 
“works period” (“cyfnod y gwaith”) means that 
period commencing at 20:00 hours on 22 October

Prohibition 
3. No person may, during the works period, drive 
any vehicle, other than an exempted vehicle, on the 
length of the trunk road west of Llangurig, Powys that 
extends from a point immediately east of the access to 
the dwelling known as Pengwaith to a point 725 
metres east of the centre-point of Pont Rhydgaled 
bridge. 
Application 
4. The prohibition in article 3 applies only during 
such times and to such extent as indicated by traffic 
signs. 
Duration of this Order 
5. The maximum duration of this Order is 18 
months.
