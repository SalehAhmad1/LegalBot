The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of a flying display rehearsal by an 
aerobatic team that is scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State, in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 of the Air Navigation 
Order 2016(a), makes the following Regulations: 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Royal Air 
Force Coningsby) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 21st October 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1230 hours and 1300 hours on 21st October 2022 no 
aircraft is to fly below 8,000 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 5 nautical miles whose centre is at 530535N 0000958W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying— 
(a) in accordance with a clearance issued the air traffic control unit at Royal Air Force 
Coningsby, or 
(b) with the permission of the Red Arrows Aerobatic Display Team Supervisor.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Royal Air Force Coningsby, 
Lincolnshire, during an air display rehearsal that is scheduled to take place there on 21st October 
2022. Many aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the display and therefore 
the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be 
restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the Red Arrows Aerobatic Display 
Team Supervisor may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
