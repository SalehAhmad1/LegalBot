The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3 by reason of the intended movement of the aircraft carrier HMS 
Queen Elizabeth. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Loch Long and 
the Firth of Clyde) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 12th March 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3 the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Loch Long and the Firth of 
Clyde by reason of the intended movement of the aircraft carrier HMS Queen Elizabeth that is 
scheduled to take place there between 12th March 2022 and 20th March 2022 (inclusive). The 
Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in 
the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(4)(b) the Police Scotland Co-ordinator at 
Glasgow Airport may be contacted on telephone number 01786 895840 or by email 
OSDAviationSafety@scotland.police.uk. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3 are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same as 
Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
