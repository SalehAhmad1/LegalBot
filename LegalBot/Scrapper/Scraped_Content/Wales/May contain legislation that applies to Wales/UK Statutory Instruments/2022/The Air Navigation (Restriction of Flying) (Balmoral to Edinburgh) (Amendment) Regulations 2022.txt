The Secretary of State has previously decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict 
flying in certain areas by reason of arrangements associated with the death of Her late Majesty 
Queen Elizabeth II and now considers it necessary in the public interest to change the specific 
times of these restrictions. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as The Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Balmoral to 
Edinburgh) (Amendment) Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 11th September 2022. 
Amendment of the Regulations 
2. The Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Balmoral to Edinburgh) Regulations 2022(b) are 
amended as follows— 
(a) in regulation 3(2) for “0930 hours and 1100 hours” substitute “0900 hours and 1215 
hours”, 
(b) in regulation 3(3) for “1030 hours and 1130 hours” substitute “0945 hours and 1230 
hours”, 
(c) in regulation 3(4) for “1100 hours and 1230 hours” substitute “1000 hours and 1330 
hours”, 
(d) in regulation 3(5) for “1145 hours and 1345 hours” substitute “1040 hours and 1500 
hours”, 
(e) in regulation 3(6) for “1315 hours and 1415 hours”, substitute “1200 hours and 1540 
hours”, 
(f) in regulation 3(7) for “1340 hours and 1500 hours”, substitute “1240 hours and 1615 
hours”, 
(g) in regulation 3(8) for “1415 hours and 1615 hours”, substitute “1315 hours and 1700

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations amend the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Balmoral to Edinburgh) 
Regulations 2022 (S.I. 2022/953). They amend the specific times of the restrictions applying on 
11th September 2022 and extend the restriction over Edinburgh from 1845 hours on 11th 
September to 2359 hours local time on 13th September 2022. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
