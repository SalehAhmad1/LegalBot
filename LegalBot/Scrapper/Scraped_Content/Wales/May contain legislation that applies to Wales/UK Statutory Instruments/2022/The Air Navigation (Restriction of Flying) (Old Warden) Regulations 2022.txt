The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
Old Warden Aerodrome, Bedfordshire by reason of the intended holding of flying displays that are 
scheduled to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes these Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 
of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Old Warden) 
Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 4th April 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(3) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace

fly below 3,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by— 
(a) the clockwise arc of a circle having a radius of 3 nautical miles whose centre is at

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Old Warden Aerodrome, 
Buckinghamshire during flying displays that are scheduled to take place there between 4th and 8th 
April 2022 (inclusive) and on 1st and 14th May 2022, 5th and 18th June 2022, 3rd and 16th July 
2022, 7th and 20th August 2022, 4th September 2022, and 2nd October 2022. 
A large number of aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the displays and 
therefore the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should 
be restricted in the vicinity of the displays for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(3) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(4), the Old Warden Aerodrome flying 
display director may be contacted on telephone number 01767 627918 or 07875028876. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
