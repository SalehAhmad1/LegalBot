The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of events 
associated with the New Year celebrations which will be attended by a large number of persons. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Edinburgh) 
Regulations 2022 and they come into force on 31st December 2022. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Edinburgh for a number of events associated with the New Year celebrations which will be 
attended by a large number of persons. The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for 
Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the 
events for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Police Scotland Aviation Safety and 
Security Unit can be contacted via OSDAviationSafety@scotland.pnn.police.uk or if urgent on 
telephone number 07815008219. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
