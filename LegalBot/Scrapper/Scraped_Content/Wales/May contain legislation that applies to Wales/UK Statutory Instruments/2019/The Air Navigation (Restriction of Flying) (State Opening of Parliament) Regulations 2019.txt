The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
vicinity of central London by reason of the holding of the State Opening of Parliament. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (State Opening 
of Parliament) Regulations 2019 and come into force on 14th October 2019. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the time referred to is Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0930 hours and 1130 hours on 14th October 2019, no 
aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 2.5 nautical miles whose centre is at 513030N 0000731W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of the National Police Air Service, 
(b) operated by or on behalf of the Helicopter Emergency Medical Services, 
(c) operated by or on behalf of the Maritime and Coastguard Agency, 
(d) making an approach to Runway 09 at London City Airport at a height of not less than 
1,500 feet above mean sea level and to the east of a line running true north to true south 
through 513030N 0000514W (London Bridge) whilst under the control of— 
(i) London City Airport Air Traffic Control, or 
(ii) London Terminal Control, Swanwick, or,

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the airspace above central London specified in 
regulation 3(1) during the State Opening of Parliament that is scheduled to take place there on 
14th October 2019. In view of the high visibility of this event and the presence of Her Majesty the 
Queen, and other VIPs, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that 
flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
In relation to regulation 3, the term ‘aircraft’ includes aircraft as classified by Part 1 of Schedule 4 
to the Air Navigation Order 2016. It also includes, by virtue of article 23 of that Order, any small 
balloon, any kite weighing not more than two kilograms, any small unmanned aircraft and any 
parachute including a parascending parachute. 
The times mentioned in this instrument are Universal Co-ordinated Time (UTC), which for the 
purpose of these Regulations is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(f), Inspector Dean Akinola may be 
contacted on telephone number 07795 267110. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice To Airmen (NOTAM).
