The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
vicinity of Birkhall, Royal Deeside, Aberdeenshire, having regard to the security considerations 
associated with this location by reason of it being the residence of members of the Royal Family. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Birkhall) 
(Restricted Zone EG R705) Regulations 2019 and come into force on 1st December 2019. 
Restricted airspace 
2.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), no aircraft is to fly below 3,500 feet above mean sea level 
within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 1 nautical mile whose centre is at 570144N 
0030427W. 
(2) Regulation 2(1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of— 
(i) Police Scotland, 
(ii) the Scottish Ambulance Service, 
(iii) the Maritime and Coastguard Agency for the purposes of a Search and Rescue 
operation, or 
(iv) The Queen’s Helicopter Flight, 
(b) flying with the permission of the Police Scotland Royalty and VIP Planning North Unit or 
the Metropolitan Police, Royalty and Specialist Protection, or 
(c) either— 
(i) operated by a member of the Royal Family, or 
(ii) landing in the grounds of Birkhall at the invitation of the person in charge of the 
household there, provided that the Police Scotland Royalty and VIP Planning North

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Birkhall, Royal Deeside, 
Aberdeenshire, in view of the need for security for the members of the Royal Family staying at, or 
visiting, Birkhall and at the request of the security services, the Secretary of State considers that it 
is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of that location. 
In relation to regulation 2, the term ‘aircraft’ includes aircraft as classified by Part 1 of Schedule 4 
to the Air Navigation Order 2016. It also includes, by virtue of article 23 of that Order, any small 
balloon, any kite weighing not more than two kilograms, any small unmanned aircraft and any 
parachute including a parascending parachute. 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulations 2(2)(b) and 2(2)(c)(ii), the Police Scotland 
Royalty and VIP Planning North Unit may be contacted on telephone number 01224 305686 and 
at OSDRoyaltyVIPPlanNorth@scotland.pnn.police.uk and the Metropolitan Police, Royalty and 
Specialist Protection may be contacted on telephone number 01953 425699. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be included in the United Kingdom Aeronautical 
Information Publication. The restricted zone has been designated as EG R705 for the purpose of 
inclusion in that publication and in related charts and documents.
