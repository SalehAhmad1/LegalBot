The Secretary of State for a reason affecting the public interest deems it necessary to restrict flying 
in the area specified in the Schedule by reason of an emergency having arisen in that area. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Liverpool) 
(Emergency) Regulations 2024 and come into force with immediate effect. 
Restricted airspace 
2.—(1) No unmanned aircraft is to fly below 2,000 feet above mean sea level over the area 
specified in Column 1 of the Schedule, being an area bounded by a circle of the radius specified in 
Column 2 and centred on the position specified in Column 3. 
(2) These Regulations do not apply to any unmanned aircraft flying in accordance with 
directions given by— 
(a) The Emergency Controlling Authority who may be contacted on telephone number 0151 
777 7939, or 
(b) the air traffic control unit at Liverpool John Lennon Airport.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions of an emergency nature on flying in the area specified in the 
Schedule. They are intended to be of temporary duration and will be revoked as soon as the 
emergency in questions has passed.
