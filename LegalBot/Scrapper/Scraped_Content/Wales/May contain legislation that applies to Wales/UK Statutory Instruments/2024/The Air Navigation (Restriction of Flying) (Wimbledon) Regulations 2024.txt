The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended gathering there of a large number of 
people at the 2024 Wimbledon Lawn Tennis Championships. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Wimbledon) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 29th June 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0500 hours and 2200 hours on each of the seventeen 
days beginning on 29th June 2024 and ending on 15th July 2024 no aircraft is to fly below 1,500 
feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 0.75 nautical 
miles centred at 512601N 0001250W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of the— 
(i) National Police Air Service, or 
(ii) Helicopter Emergency Medical Services, 
(b) making an approach to or departing from London Heathrow Airport whilst under the 
direction of the London Terminal Control Centre, Swanwick or the air traffic control unit 
at London Heathrow Airport, or 
(c) flying in accordance with the permission of the Championship Safety Officer.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of The All England Lawn Tennis 
Club, Wimbledon on the occasion of the 2024 Wimbledon Lawn Tennis Championships which are 
due to take place there from 29th June 2024 to 15th July 2024 (inclusive). 
As large crowds are expected to attend this event each day, the Championships Safety Officer has 
requested that restrictions be imposed on flying in the vicinity of the Championships on public 
safety and security grounds. This has been agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and the 
Department for Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Championship Safety Officer may 
be contacted as notified.
