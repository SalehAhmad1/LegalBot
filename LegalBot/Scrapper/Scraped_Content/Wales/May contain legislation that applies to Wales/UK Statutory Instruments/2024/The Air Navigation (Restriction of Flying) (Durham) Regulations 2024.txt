The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intending holding there of the 
Durham Miners’ Gala. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Durham) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 13th July 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 0400 hours and 2200 hours on 13th July 2024 no 
unmanned aircraft is to fly below 1,400 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a 
circle having a radius of 1.2 nautical miles whose centre is at 544627N 0013346W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply— 
(a) insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) 
(Prisons and Young Offender Institutions) (England and Wales) Regulations 2023(c), or 
(b) to any unmanned aircraft flying in accordance with a permission issued by the Durham 
Constabulary.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Durham, during the Durham Miners’ Gala that is due to take place there on 13th July 2024. At the 
request of the Durham Constabulary, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for 
Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of that 
event for reasons of public safety and security. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Prisons and Young Offender Institutions) (England and Wales) 
Regulations 2023 (S.I. 2023/1101). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the Durham Constabulary may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
