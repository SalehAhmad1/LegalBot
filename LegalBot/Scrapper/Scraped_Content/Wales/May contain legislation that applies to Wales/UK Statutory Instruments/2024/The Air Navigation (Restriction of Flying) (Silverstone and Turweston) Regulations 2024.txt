The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) and 4(1) by reason of the intended holding there of the 2024 
British Grand Prix. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Silverstone and 
Turweston) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 4th July 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(2) and 4(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Silverstone motor-racing circuit 
and Heliport and Turweston Aerodrome on the occasion of the 2024 British Grand Prix that is due 
to take place there between 4th July 2024 and 7th July 2024 (inclusive). 
A significant number of helicopters will be engaged in the transport of spectators to and from the 
event in addition to very large crowds that will also be attending the event. Accordingly, the Civil 
Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event location for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) and 4(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is 
one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
