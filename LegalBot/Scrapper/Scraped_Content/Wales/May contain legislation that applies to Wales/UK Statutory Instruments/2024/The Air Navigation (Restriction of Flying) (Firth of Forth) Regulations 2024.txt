The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest and the interests of national 
defence to restrict the flying of unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason 
of the operations of HMS Queen Elizabeth which are due to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Firth of Forth) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 21st March 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of the 
Firth of Forth due to the operations of HMS Queen Elizabeth that are due to take place there on 
21st March 2024 to 25th March 2024, inclusive. At the request of the Police Scotland, the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft 
should be restricted in the vicinity of the Firth of Forth for reasons of national defence and the 
public interest. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Nuclear Installations) Regulations 2016 (S.I. 2016/1003) and are 
applicable particularly to EGR603 ROSYTH. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the Police Scotland Aviation Safety 
and Security Unit may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
