The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of flying displays there by a jet 
formation display team. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Jet Formation 
Display Teams) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 31st May 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), during the period commencing at 0800 hours and ending at 
2000 hours on the corresponding date or dates specified in column 4 of the Schedule, no aircraft is 
to fly below the corresponding altitude or flight level(c) specified in column 3 of the Schedule 
within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 6 nautical miles centred at the point 
specified in column 2 of the Schedule. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft flying— 
(a) within controlled airspace or within an aerodrome traffic zone in respect of which there is 
an appropriate air traffic control unit(d) if it flies in accordance with a clearance or 
permission of the unit controlling that airspace or zone, or 
(b) in accordance with a permission issued by the relevant flying display director. 
 
 
 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations. 
(b) Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
(c) The term “flight level” is defined in Schedule 1 to the Air Navigation Order 2016. 
(d) The term “air traffic control unit” is defined in Schedule 1 to the Air Navigation Order 2016.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in various locations as specified in the Schedule 
during flying displays by jet formation teams such as the Royal Air Force Aerobatic Team, the 
Red Arrows, which are due to take place during the period from 31st May 2024 to 5th October 
2024 (inclusive). 
 
3
In order to protect the display aircraft, other air traffic and spectators, the Civil Aviation Authority 
and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the 
airspace within which the display teams will perform for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the relevant flying display director 
may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
