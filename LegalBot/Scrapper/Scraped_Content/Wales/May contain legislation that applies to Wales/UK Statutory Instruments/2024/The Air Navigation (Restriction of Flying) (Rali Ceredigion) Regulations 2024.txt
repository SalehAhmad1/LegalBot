The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the holding of the Rali Ceredigion which is due to 
take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Rali 
Ceredigion) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 30th August 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), during the periods specified in column 4 of the Schedule, no 
aircraft is to fly below the corresponding altitude specified in column 3 of the Schedule within the 
area bounded by a circle having a radius specified in column 2 of the Schedule centred at the point 
specified in column 1 of the Schedule. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) flying in accordance with a permission issued by the Rali Ceredigion helicopter co-
ordinator, or 
(b) operated by or on behalf of the— 
(i) National Police Air Service, 
(ii) Helicopter Emergency Medical Services, or 
(iii) Maritime and Coastguard Agency. 
 
 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of aircraft in the vicinity of a number of sites 
due to the holding of the Rali Ceredigion that is scheduled to take place from 30th August to 1st 
September 2024, inclusive. 
In view of the large number of spectators attending, the fixed wing, rotary wing and unmanned 
aircraft involved in both the filming of the rally and the relay of broadcast data, and the transport 
of rally officials and VIP guests to and from the various stages, the Civil Aviation Authority and 
the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety.

The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) and the Schedule are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC)

In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(a), the Rali Ceredigion helicopter co-
ordinator may be contacted as notified.
