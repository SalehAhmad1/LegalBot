The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intending holding there 
of The British Fireworks Championships. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Plymouth) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 14th August 2024.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of the 
Mountbatten Breakwater, Plymouth during The British Fireworks Championships that are due to 
take place there between 14th August 2024 and 15th August 2024 (inclusive). 
At the request of the Plymouth City Council, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for 
Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the 
event location for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Plymouth City Council Senior 
Events Officer, Economic Development may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
