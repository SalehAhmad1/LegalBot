The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended gathering there 
of a large number of persons at the Creamfields music festival. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Daresbury) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 22nd August 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of the 
Creamfields music festival, Daresbury, Warrington, Cheshire by reason of the large number of 
persons expected to attend the event which is due to take place there from 22nd to 26th August 
2024, inclusive. 
The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned 
aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Event Licencing Co-ordinator may 
be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
