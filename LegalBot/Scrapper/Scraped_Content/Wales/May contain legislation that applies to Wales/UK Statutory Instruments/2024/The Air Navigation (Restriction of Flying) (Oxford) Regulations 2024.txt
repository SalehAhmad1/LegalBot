The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
areas specified in regulation 3(1) and (2) by reason of the intended holding at Blenheim Palace of 
the European Political Community meeting. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Oxford) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 18th July 2024.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Oxford, Oxfordshire, during the 
European Political Community meeting that is taking place at Blenheim Palace. 
A large number of aircraft will be both inbound and outbound from London Oxford airport as well 
as Royal Air Force Brize Norton in connection with this meeting, therefore the Civil Aviation 
Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity, 
for reasons affecting the public interest. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) and (2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is 
one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) The term “flight level” is defined in Schedule 1 to the Air Navigation Order 2016.
