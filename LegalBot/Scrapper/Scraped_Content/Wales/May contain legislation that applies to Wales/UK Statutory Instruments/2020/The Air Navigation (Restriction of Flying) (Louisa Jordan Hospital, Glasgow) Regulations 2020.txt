The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
the Scottish Event Campus, Glasgow, Renfrewshire, by reason of the establishment of the NHS 
Louisa Jordan Temporary Field Hospital at that site. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Louisa Jordan 
Hospital, Glasgow) Regulations 2020 and come into force on 16th April 2020. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1200 hours on 16th April 2020 and 1200 hours on 
14th July 2020, no aircraft is to fly below 2,000 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded 
by a circle having a radius of 2 nautical miles whose centre is 555139N 0041720W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or flying in the service of— 
(i) Police Scotland, 
(ii) the Scottish Air Ambulance Service, or 
(iii) the Maritime and Coastguard Agency for the purpose of search and rescue, medical 
emergency or NHS patient transfer, 
(b) landing at, or departing Glasgow Airport, the Police Scotland Heliport or NHS Louisa 
Jordan Hospital, Scottish Event Campus, Glasgow, whilst under the control of the air 
traffic control unit at Glasgow Airport, or

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in airspace in the vicinity of the Scottish Event 
Campus, Glasgow, Renfrewshire, by reason of the establishment of NHS Louisa Jordan 
Temporary Field Hospital there between 16 April 2020 and 14 July 2020. Due to the likelihood of 
rotary aircraft activity associated with the field hospital, the Civil Aviation Authority and the 
Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the site for 
reasons of public safety. 
In relation to regulation 3, the term ‘aircraft’ includes aircraft as classified by Part 1 of Schedule 4 
to the Air Navigation Order 2016. It also includes, by virtue of article 23 of that Order, any small 
balloon, any kite weighing not more than two kilograms, any small unmanned aircraft and any 
parachute including a parascending parachute. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC), which for the 
purpose of these Regulations is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(c), the Police Scotland Aviation Safety 
and Security Unit may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice To Airmen (NOTAM).
