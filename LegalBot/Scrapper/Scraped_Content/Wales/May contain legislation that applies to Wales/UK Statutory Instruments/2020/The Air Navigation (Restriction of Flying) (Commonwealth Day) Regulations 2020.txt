The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
Westminster Abbey, central London, by reason of the celebration of the Commonwealth of 
Nations. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) 
(Commonwealth Day) Regulations 2020 and come into force on 9th March 2020. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1200 hours and 1800 hours on 9th March 2020, no 
aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 1 nautical mile whose centre is 512957N 0000738W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of— 
(i) the National Police Air Service, 
(ii) the Helicopter Emergency Medical Services, or 
(iii) the Maritime and Coastguard Agency, 
(b) making an approach to Runway 09 at London City Airport whilst under the control of 
London City Airport Air Traffic Control or London Terminal Control Swanwick, 
(c) making an approach to Runway 27L/R at Heathrow Airport whilst under the control of 
Heathrow Airport Air Traffic Control or London Terminal Control Swanwick, or 
(d) flying in accordance with the permission of the Metropolitan Police Service.

(b) In these Regulations, Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is the same as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the airspace above central London during the 
Commonwealth Day Service at Westminster Abbey that is scheduled to take place there on 9th 
March 2020. In view of the high visibility of this event and the presence of members of the Royal 
Family, and other VIPs, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that 
flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
In relation to regulation 3, the term ‘aircraft’ includes aircraft as classified by Part 1 of Schedule 4 
to the Air Navigation Order 2016. It also includes, by virtue of article 23 of that Order, any small 
balloon, any kite weighing not more than two kilograms, any small unmanned aircraft and any 
parachute including a parascending parachute. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC), which for the 
purpose of these Regulations is the same as Greenwich Mean Time. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(d), the Metropolitan Police Service may 
be contacted as notified in the Aeronautical Information Publication. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice To Airmen (NOTAM).
