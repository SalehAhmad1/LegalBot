The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
East Kirkby, Lincolnshire, by reason of the holding of flying displays. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (East Kirkby, 
Lincolnshire) Regulations 2021 and come into force on 7th August 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), between 1130 hours and 1630 hours on 7th August 2021 no 
aircraft is to fly below 3,000 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having 
a radius of 1.5 nautical miles whose centre is at 530811N 0000019E. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any aircraft— 
(a) flying in accordance with a permission issued by the East Kirkby Air Show flying display 
director, or 
(b) making an approach to, or departing from, Royal Air Force Coningsby under the control 
of the air traffic control unit at Royal Air Force Coningsby.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of East Kirkby, Lincolnshire, 
during air displays that are scheduled to take place there on 7th August 2021. Many aircraft will 
be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the displays and therefore the Civil Aviation 
Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the 
event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(a), the East Kirkby Air Show flying 
display director may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Airmen (NOTAM). 
The point of contact in the Civil Aviation Authority is Mr S J Maxted, Airspace Regulator, Safety 
and Airspace Regulation Group, on telephone number 03301 383216.
