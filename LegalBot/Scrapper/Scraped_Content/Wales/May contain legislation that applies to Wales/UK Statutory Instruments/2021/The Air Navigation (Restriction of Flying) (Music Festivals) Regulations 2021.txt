The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft by reason of the intended gathering of large numbers of persons at music festivals in the 
vicinity of various sites in England. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Music 
Festivals) Regulations 2021 and come into force on 21st July 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of Beccles 
(regulation 3(1)(a)), Lulworth Castle (regulation 3(1)(b)), Cornbury Park Oxfordshire (regulation 
3(1)(c)), Reading (regulation 3(1)(d)), Bramham Estate Leeds (regulation 3(1)(e)), Daresbury 
Estate Cheshire (regulation 3(1)(f)), Clapham Common London (regulation 3(1)(g)), Crystal 
Palace Park London (regulation 3(1)(h)), Heaton Park Manchester (regulation 3(1)(i)), Isle of 
Wight (regulation 3(1)(j)), and Pier Head Liverpool (regulation 3(1)(k)) by reason of the large 
numbers of persons attending the music festivals which are scheduled to be held there during July, 
August and September 2021. The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport 
agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted at each festival for reasons of public 
safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Festival Airspace Co-ordinator may 
be contacted on telephone number 03302210662. 
Full details of the Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM). 
The point of contact in the Civil Aviation Authority is Mr S J Maxted, Airspace Regulator, Safety 
and Airspace Regulation Group, on telephone number 03301 383216.
