The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
vicinity of Silverstone motor-racing circuit and Heliport and Turweston Aerodrome by reason of 
the holding of the British Grand Prix 2021. 
The Secretary of State makes these Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 
of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Silverstone and 
Turweston) Regulations 2021 and come into force on 16th July 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(2) and 4(2) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Silverstone motor-racing circuit 
and Heliport and Turweston Aerodrome on the occasion of the British Grand Prix 2021 event that 
is scheduled to take place between 16th and 18th July 2021. 
A significant number of helicopters will be engaged in the transport of spectators to and from the 
event in addition to very large crowds that will also be attracted to the event. Accordingly, the 
Civil Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) and regulation 4(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time 
(UTC) which is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
