The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
Portsmouth Harbour, Hampshire by reason of the intended holding of a flying display there by a 
jet formation display team. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Portsmouth 
Harbour) Regulations 2021 and they come into force on 20th October 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Portsmouth Harbour, Hampshire 
during flying displays by the Royal Air Force Aerobatic Team, the Red Arrows, which are due to 
take place there on 20th October 2021 with the 21st October 2021 being the reserve date for the 
displays. In order to protect the display aircraft, other air traffic and spectators, it is considered that 
flying should be restricted in the vicinity of the airspace within which the display team will 
perform. The restrictions have been agreed by the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for 
Transport. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Royal Air Force Aerobatic Team 
Supervisor or the Flying Display Director may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
