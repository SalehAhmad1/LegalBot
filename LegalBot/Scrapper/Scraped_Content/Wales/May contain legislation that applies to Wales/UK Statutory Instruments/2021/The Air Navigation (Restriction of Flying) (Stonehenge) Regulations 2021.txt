The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the vicinity of 
Stonehenge, Wiltshire, by reason of the intended gathering of a large number of persons for the 
Summer and Winter Solstice events scheduled to be held there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Stonehenge) 
Regulations 2021 and come into force on 20th June 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Stonehenge, Wiltshire, during 
the summer and winter solstice events during which it is anticipated there will be large gatherings 
of persons on 20th and 21st June 2021 and 21st December and 22nd December 2021. For security 
and public safety reasons, Wiltshire Police has requested the Secretary of State to restrict flying in 
the vicinity of Stonehenge during these periods. This has been agreed with the Civil Aviation 
Authority and the Department for Transport. 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), Wiltshire Police may be contacted as 
notified. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST) in regulation 3(1)(a) and is the same time as Greenwich Mean 
Time (GMT) in regulation 3(1)(b). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Airmen (NOTAM).
