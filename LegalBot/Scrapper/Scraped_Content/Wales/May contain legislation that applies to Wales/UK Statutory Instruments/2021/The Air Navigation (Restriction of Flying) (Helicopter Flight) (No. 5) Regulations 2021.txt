The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
vicinity of a route between Balmoral and Edinburgh by reason of two intended helicopter flights. 
The Secretary of State makes these Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 
of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Helicopter 
Flight) (No. 5) Regulations 2021 and they come into force on 3rd September 2021. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Balmoral and Edinburgh by 
reason of two intended helicopter Flights. The Civil Aviation Authority and Department for 
Transport agree that flying in the area of these intended helicopter flights should be restricted for 
security and flight safety reasons to avoid the risk that transient aircraft may disrupt them.  
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
