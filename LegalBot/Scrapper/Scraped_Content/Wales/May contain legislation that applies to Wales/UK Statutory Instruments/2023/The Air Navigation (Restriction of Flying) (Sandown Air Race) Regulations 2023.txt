The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of an Aero Club Air Race. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Sandown Air 
Race) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 23rd September 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(2) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (3), during the periods specified in paragraph (2) no aircraft is to 
fly below 1,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by straight lines joining 
successively the following points— 
(a) 504524N 0011717W, 
(b) 504727N 0010830W, 
(c) 504653N 0005825W, 
(d) 504244N 0005808W, and 
(e) 504524N 0011717W. 
(2) The periods referred to in paragraph (1) are between— 
(a) 0930 hours and 1300 hours on 23rd September 2023, 
(b) 1400 hours and 1730 hours on 23rd September 2023, 
(c) 1000 hours and 1300 hours on 24th September 2023, and 
(d) 1400 hours and 1700 hours on 24th September 2023. 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations. 
(b) Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Sandown, Isle of Wight during 
the intended holding of an Aero Club Air Race that is due to take place there on 23rd September 
2023 and 24th September 2023. 
Many aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the race and therefore the Civil 
Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 3(3)(a), the Sandown Aerodrome air ground 
service and the Royal Aero Club Records Racing and Rally Association Clerk of the Course may 
be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
