The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict the flying of unmanned 
aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the holding of the Eurovision Song 
Contest which is intended to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Eurovision 
Song Contest) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 4th May 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), no unmanned aircraft is to fly below 1,200 feet above mean sea 
level— 
(a) between 2300 hours on the 4th May 2023 and 0500 hours on the 14th May 2023 within 
the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 0.5 nautical miles centred at 532350N 
0025929W, 
(b) between 2300 hours on the 4th May 2023 and 0600 hours on the 14th May 2023 within 
the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 0.5 nautical miles centred at 532417N 
0025948W, 
(c) between 0600 hours on the 7th May 2023 and 0600 hours on the 8th May 2023 within the 
area bounded by a circle having a radius of 0.5 nautical miles centred at 532431N 
0025848W, and 
(d) between 0600 hours on the 13th May 2023 and 0600 hours on the 14th May 2023 within 
the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 0.5 nautical miles centred at 532340N 
0025850W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Liverpool, Merseyside due to the holding of the Eurovision Song Contest that is scheduled to take 
place there. In view of the high visibility of this event and at the request of the Merseyside Police 
Service, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that the flying of 
unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and 
security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the Merseyside Police Service 
operational planning department may be contacted on telephone number 101. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Briefing Sheet and Notice to 
Aviation (NOTAM).
