The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding there of flying displays 
associated with the Eastbourne International Air Show. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Eastbourne) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 16th August 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(2) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (3), during the periods specified in paragraph (2) no aircraft is to 
fly below 5,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by straight lines joining 
successively the following points— 
(a) 504506N 0000930E, 
(b) 505038N 0001835E, 
(c) 504619N 0002508E, 
(d) 504047N 0001603E, and 
(e) 504506N 0000930E. 
(2) The periods referred to in paragraph (1) are— 
(a) between 1200 hours and 1600 hours on 16th August 2022, and 
(b) between 1100 hours and 1700 hours on each of the four days from 17th August 2023 to 
20th August 2023. 
                                                                                                                                            
(a) S.I. 2016/765, to which there are amendments not relevant to these Regulations.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Eastbourne, East Sussex during 
flying displays associated with the Eastbourne International Air Show that are due to take place 
there from 16th August 2023 to 20th August 2023 (inclusive). 
Many aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the displays and therefore the 
Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in 
the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3)(a), the Eastbourne International Air 
Show flying display director may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
