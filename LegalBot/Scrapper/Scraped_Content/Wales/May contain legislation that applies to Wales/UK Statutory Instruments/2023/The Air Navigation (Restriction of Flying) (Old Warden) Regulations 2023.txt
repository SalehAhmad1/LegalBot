The Secretary of State has decided that it is in the public interest to restrict flying in the area 
specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intended holding of flying displays that are scheduled 
to take place there. 
The Secretary of State makes these Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by article 239 
of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Old Warden) 
Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 7th May 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(2) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Old Warden Aerodrome, 
Buckinghamshire during flying displays for the general public that are scheduled to take place 
there on 7th May 2023, 20th May 2023, 4th June 2023, 17th June 2023, 2nd July 2023, 15th July 
2023, 6th August 2023, 19th August 2023, 3rd September 2023 and 1st October 2023. 
A large number of aircraft will be involved in high energy manoeuvres during the displays and 
therefore the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should 
be restricted in the vicinity of the display for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(2) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(3), the Old Warden Aerodrome flying 
display director may be contacted on telephone numbers 01767 627918 or 07875 028876. Old 
Warden Aerodrome may be also contacted on frequency 130.705MHz. 
The point of contact in the Civil Aviation Authority is Mr David Ridley, Airspace Regulator, 
Safety and Airspace Regulation Group, on telephone number 0330 1382471.
