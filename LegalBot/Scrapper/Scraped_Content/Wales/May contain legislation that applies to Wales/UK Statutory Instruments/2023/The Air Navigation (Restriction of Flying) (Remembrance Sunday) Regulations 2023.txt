The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the holding there of the Remembrance Sunday 
ceremony at the Cenotaph. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Remembrance 
Sunday) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 12th November 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace

no aircraft is to fly below 2,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle 
having a radius of 2.5 nautical miles centred at 513009N 0000734W.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying above Central London during the Remembrance 
Sunday ceremony at the Cenotaph which is due to take place there on 12th November 2023. 
The Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for Transport agree that flying should be 
restricted in the vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety and security. 
These Regulations apply except insofar as flying is already prohibited by the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (City of London) Regulations 2004 (S.I. 2004/2092), the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Specified Area) Regulations 2005 (S.I. 2005/964), and the Air Navigation 
(Restriction of Flying) (Hyde Park) Regulations 2017 (S.I. 2017/1300). 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is the same 
time as Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(c), the Metropolitan Police Service may 
be contacted on telephone number 07775 010510. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular
