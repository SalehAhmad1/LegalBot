The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict flying in the 
area specified in regulations 3(1) and 4(1) by reason of the intended holding there of the 2023 
British Grand Prix. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Silverstone and 
Turweston) Regulations 2023 and they come into force on 7th July 2023. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulations 3(2) and 4(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b).

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of Silverstone motor-racing circuit 
and Heliport and Turweston Aerodrome on the occasion of the British Grand Prix international 
race car meeting that is scheduled to take place there between 7th July 2023 and 9th July 2023 
(inclusive). 
A significant number of helicopters will be engaged in the transport of spectators to and from the 
event in addition to very large crowds that are due to attend the event. Accordingly, the Civil 
Aviation Authority and Department for Transport agree that flying should be restricted in the 
vicinity of the event for reasons of public safety. 
The times mentioned in regulations 3(2) and 4(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which 
is one hour behind British Summer Time (BST). 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
