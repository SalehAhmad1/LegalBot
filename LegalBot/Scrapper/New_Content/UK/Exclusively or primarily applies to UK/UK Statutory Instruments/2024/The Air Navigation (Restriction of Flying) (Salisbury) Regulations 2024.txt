The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the area specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intending gathering of a 
large number of persons in the vicinity of Salisbury, Wiltshire. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Salisbury) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 14th October 2024. 
Interpretation 
2. In regulation 3(1) the times referred to are Co-ordinated Universal Time(b). 
Restricted airspace 
3.—(1) Subject to paragraph (2), for the period beginning with 0600 hours on 14th October 
2024 and ending with 1700 hours on 25th October 2024 no unmanned aircraft is to fly below 
1,500 feet above mean sea level within the area bounded by a circle having a radius of 0.25 of a 
nautical mile whose centre is at 510409N 0014740W. 
(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any unmanned aircraft— 
(a) operated by or on behalf of the— 
(i) National Police Air Service, 
(ii) Wiltshire Police Service, or 
(b) flying in accordance with a permission issued by the Wiltshire Police Service.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of the 
Guildhall, Salisbury, Wiltshire, where the gathering of a large number of persons is due to take 
place from 14th to 25th October 2024, inclusive. 
At the request of the Wiltshire Police, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Department for 
Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the vicinity for reasons 
of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2)(b), the Wiltshire Police Service may be 
contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
