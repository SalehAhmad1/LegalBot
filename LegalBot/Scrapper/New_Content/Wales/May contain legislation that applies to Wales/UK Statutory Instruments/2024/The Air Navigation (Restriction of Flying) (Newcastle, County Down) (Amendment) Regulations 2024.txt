The Secretary of State has deemed it necessary in the public interest to amend the restrictions on 
flying specified in the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Newcastle, County Down) 
Regulations 2024(a). 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(b). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Newcastle, 
County Down) (Amendment) Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 9th September 2024. 
Restricted airspace 
2.—(1) The Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Newcastle, County Down) Regulations 2024 
are amended as follows. 
(2) In regulation 2 for “3(1)” substitute “3”. 
(3) For regulation 3(2) substitute— 
“(2) Paragraph (1) does not apply to any unmanned aircraft flying in accordance with a 
permission issued by the— 
(a) Amgen Irish Open event Safety Officer for the period— 
 
(i) beginning with 0400 hours on 9th September 2024 and ending with 0529 
hours on 11th September 2024, and 
 
(ii) beginning with 1831 hours on 15th September and ending with 2100 hours on 
16th September, or 
(b) Police Service of Northern Ireland, for the period beginning with 0530 hours on 
11th September 2024 and ending with 1830 hours on 15th September 2024.”.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations amend the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Newcastle, County Down) 
Regulations 2024 (S.I. 2024/675) which impose restrictions on flying in the vicinity of the Amgen 
Irish Open Golf Championships, Newcastle, County Down 
In relation to the permissions mentioned in regulation 2(3), the Amgen Irish Open event Safety 
Officer and the Police Service of Northern Ireland may be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by a Notice to Aviation (NOTAM). 
The point of contact in the Civil Aviation Authority is Mr Alan Frew, Airspace Regulator, Safety 
and Airspace Regulation Group, on telephone number 03301 382734.
