The Secretary of State has decided that it is necessary in the public interest to restrict the flying of 
unmanned aircraft in the areas specified in regulation 3(1) by reason of the intending holding there 
of multi-agency exercises. 
The Secretary of State makes the following Regulations in exercise of the powers conferred by 
article 239 of the Air Navigation Order 2016(a). 
Citation and commencement 
1. These Regulations may be cited as the Air Navigation (Restriction of Flying) (Shropshire) 
Regulations 2024 and they come into force on 8th October 2024.

EXPLANATORY NOTE 
(This note is not part of the Regulations) 
These Regulations impose restrictions on the flying of unmanned aircraft in the vicinity of 
Edgmond, Lilleshall and Shrewsbury in Shropshire, where multi-agency exercises are intended to 
take place from 8th to 10th October 2024, inclusive. 
At the request of the West Midlands Police Service, the Civil Aviation Authority and the 
Department for Transport agree that the flying of unmanned aircraft should be restricted in the 
vicinity of those exercises for reasons of public safety and security. 
The times mentioned in regulation 3(1) are Co-ordinated Universal Time (UTC) which is one hour 
behind British Summer Time (BST). 
In relation to the permission mentioned in regulation 3(2), the West Midlands Police Service may 
be contacted as notified. 
Full details of this Statutory Instrument will be promulgated by Aeronautical Information Circular 
(AIC) and Notice to Aviation (NOTAM).
